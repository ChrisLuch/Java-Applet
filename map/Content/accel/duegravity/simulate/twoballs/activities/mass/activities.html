ACTIVITIES FOR THE TWO-BALL APPLET

ACTIVITY 1: The Effect of Mass on Projectile Motion

(a) Negligible Air Resistance

Place both balls at the origin. Set the mass of Ball 2 (m2) to 0.3 kg. The mass of Ball 1 (m1) is set to 0.1 kg by the applet.

Make the launch speeds and angles are equal by setting v1x = v2x = v1y = v2y = 20.0 m/s.

Make sure that the air resistance is set to zero.

Launch the balls, observe their trajectories, and answer the following questions.

The "range" is the horizontal displacement of the projectile (ball) when it has fallen back down to the initial horizontal level, and the "time of flight" is the corresponding time elapsed.

1. Which of the following expressions relating their ranges is true?

(a) R1 = R2
(b) R1 < R2
(c) R1 < R2

2. Which of the following expressions relating their times of flights corresponding to respective ranges is true

(a) t1 < t2
(b) t1 = t2
(c) t1 < t2

3. Which of the following expressions relating their maximum heights is true?

(a) H1 = H2
(b) H1 < H2
(c) H1 > H2


(b) Non-Zero Air Resistance

Make the same initial settings as for case (a) above, but set the air resistance constant C (for Ball 2) to 0.0020 kg/m. The air resistance constant for Ball 1 is preset by the applet to zero.

Launch the balls, observe their trajectories, and answer the questions from the previous activity again.
