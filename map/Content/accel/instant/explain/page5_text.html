Acceleration

The simulation shows Velocity Space, i.e., the space of velocity vectors. The vector shown is a velocity vector whose tail end is fixed at the origin of Velocity Space. Imagine this vector to be the velocity of some object.

You will be able to control the way this velocity vector changes by adjusting an acceleration vector in the acceleration dial.

First set the velocity vector to the initial value you want by dragging the tip of the vector.

Then pick an acceleration by clicking inside the acceleration dial. An acceleration vector will be drawn from the center of the dial to the point where you clicked. The velocity vector will be changing accordingly, i.e., its tip will be moving in the direction of the acceleration vector at a rate proportional to the magnitude of the acceleration vector. To change the acceleration, keep the mouse button depressed and drag the tip of the acceleration vector.

Can you observe that the relationship between the velocity and acceleration vectors is exactly like that between the position and velocity vectors? For a review of the latter, go to Velocity/Basic Facts/Simulate It. In fact, just imagine that the labels 'Velocity Space' and 'Acceleration Dial' are changed to 'Position Space' and 'Velocity Dial', respectively, and the present simulation performs exactly like the simulation in Velocity/Basic Facts/Simulate It.

Basic Lesson

As the time-rate-of-change of the position vector gives the velocity vector, so the time-rate-of-change of the velocity vector gives the acceleration vector.

Some details on controlling the applet

While steering the velocity from the acceleration dial: when you release the mouse button, the velocity vector's motion will be frozen. Clicking in the acceleration dial again, will resume the motion with the new acceleration, as long as the mouse button is held down.

If the velocity vector extends to one of the boundaries of velocity space, the motion will be frozen. You can start it again, by changing the acceleration appropriately or by dragging the tip of the velocity vector to a new position and then setting the acceleration in the acceleration dial.
