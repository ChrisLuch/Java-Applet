(d) Constant Velocity Motion

Yes, motion (d) is the motion in which the object is not accelerating.

To start the motion, click anywhere in the simulation window. To reset, click a second time and then click once more to start the motion again.

The object is moving in a straight line with constant speed. That means it is moving with constant velocity.

The acceleration is zero if and only if the velocity is constant. When the velocity is changing, either in magnitude or direction, the acceleration is unequal to zero.
