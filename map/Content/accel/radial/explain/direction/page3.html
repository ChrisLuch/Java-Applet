Direction of the Acceleration in Uniform Circular Motion

The definition of velocity as time-rate-of-change of position implies that

the direction of the velocity vector at a given instant coincides with the direction in which the tip of the position vector is moving at that instant, which means that the velocity vector when attached to the tip of the position vector lies along the tangent to the particle's path at the given instant.

The analogous definition of acceleration as time-rate-of-change of velocity implies that

the direction of the acceleration vector at a given instant coincides with the direction in which the tip of the velocity vector is moving at that instant, where the tail end of the velocity vector must be held fixed as in Pair 2.

The velocity vector must have its tail end fixed just like the position vector has its tail end fixed. Otherwise, the motion of the tip of the velocity vector would not represent the changes D in the velocity vector in the definition of acceleration, just as the motion of the tip of the position vector represents the changes D in the position vector in the definition of velocity.

The image at the top shows the path (magenta) traced by the tip of the velocity vector in Pair 2. For constant speed v, this velocity path is a circle just like the position path (blue) traced by the tip of the position vector. The acceleration vector at a given instant must point along the tangent to the velocity path at that instant because the tip of the velocity vector is moving in the direction of the tangent. The image at the top illustrates the tangential direction of the acceleration vector.
