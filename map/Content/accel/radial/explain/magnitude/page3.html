Magnitude of the Radial Acceleration

Remember that in circular motion, illustrated above, the magnitude v of the velocity is related to the angular speed w of the position vector by

v = r w .(1)

To become comfortable with this equation, consider the special case of uniform circular motion in which w and v are constant. (Equation (1) is true in general, whether v and w are constants or not.)

For constant w, the angle covered in period T is 2p. Therefore,

w = 2p/T, w = const .(2)

Question. What does one get for v when substituting Expression (2) for w into Equ.(1)?

Answer. One obtains

v = r×2p / T = (2pr) / T .(3)

This equation is correct since 2pr is equal to the circumference of a circle of radius r so that the right-hand side is just distance traveled divided by time elapsed.
