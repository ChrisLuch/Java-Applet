Magnitude of the Radial Acceleration

Final Remarks

  • The acceleration of particle moving with constant speed v, and constant angular speed w, in a circle of radius r is in the radial direction and has a magnitude a given by the following equations.

    a = r w2 .(5)

    and

    a = v2 / r .(6)

  • If the particle's motion is circular, but not uniform, so that the speed and angular speed are changing, the direction of the particle's acceleration is no longer radial, but has an additional tangential component. The applet on Page 5 simulates this if you set the angular acceleration a or the time-rate-of-change of speed dv/dt to a non-zero value.

    For such motion, the magnitude of the radial component of the acceleration is still given by Expressions (5) and (6), but the magnitude a of the total acceleration is larger because it must reflect the presence of the non-zero tangential component also.

    Task. Go to the applet on Page 5, and set dv/dt to a non-zero value and r and v(0) to values of your choice. Play the applet, and Pause it at some point. Calculate the magnitude of the radial component of the acceleration at this instant using the necessary data from the Data box of the applet. Compare your answer for arad with that given in the Data box. A slight discrepancy would be due to round-off error because the Data box displays its data to a limited number of significant digits.

  • For general motions that are neither circular nor uniform, the acceleration can still be thought of as consisting of a radial and tangential component. The radial direction in this case refers to that of a so-called circle of curvature that can be smoothly fitted into the particle's tracjectory. The radial direction is perpendicular to the trajectory at the given point.

    Even in this most general case, the magnitude of the radial component of the acceleration is still given by Expressions (5) or (6) with r now being the radius of the circle of curvature. The applet simulates only circular motion and therefore cannot demonstrate this most general case.
