Acceleration

Select Button 2 to display the velocity (magenta) and acceleration (orange) vectors of the pendulum bob, and then click Play . If you want to make the velocity and acceleration vectors larger, drag the pendulum bob to a higher starting position before clicking Play.

As you can see, the velocity and acceleration vectors are changing all the time during the motion and, in general, point in different directions at any given instant.

This session will provide an intuitive, qualitative explanation of how velocity and acceleration are related. Both of them are vector quantities, and it is important to understand that acceleration is derived from velocity, and not from speed, which is a scalar quantity. This fact is at the core of some essential properties of acceleration.
