(b) Projectile Motion

To start the motion, click anywhere in the simulation window. To reset, click a second time. The simulation shows positions of the object equal time intervals apart.

This motion is called projectile motion. If you throw a ball into the air, it will perform approximately the kind of motion that is simulated here. (Not exactly, because of air resistance.)

In this case, the velocity is changing in two ways: both its magnitude is changing, because the distances between the positions of the object equal time intervals apart are changing, and the direction of the velocity is changing.

Whenever the velocity is changing, the acceleration is non-zero.
