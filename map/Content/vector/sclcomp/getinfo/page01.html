Scalar Components of Vectors

Vectors can be specified in two different ways:

  • in terms of magnitude (absolute value) and direction
  • in terms of scalar components.

The number of dimensions of the space the vector is in is equal to the number of scalars needed to specify a vector.

E.g., a vector in two dimensions has two scalar components. Alternatively, such a vector can be specified by its magnitude and one direction angle.

In three dimensions, one needs three scalar components, or the magnitude and two direction angles.
