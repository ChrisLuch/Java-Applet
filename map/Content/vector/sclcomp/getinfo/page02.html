Scalar Components of Vectors

The scalar components of the vector in the diagram on the left are represented by the green and yellow lines. These components, denoted ax and ay, are equal to the x and y coordinates of the tip of the vector if the tail end of the vector is placed at the origin.

In the present diagram, the tip of vector has the x and y coordinates -6.4 and 5.0, respectively. Therefore,

ax = - 6.4

ay = 5.0 .

In MAP, the following notation will be used to specify a vector in terms of its scalar components:

= (ax,ay).

Therefore the present vector can be written

= (-6.4, 5.0).
