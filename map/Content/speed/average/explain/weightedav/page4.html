Time-weighted Average of Speeds

Another example calculation

A runner runs a course that is uphill for 2 km and downhill for 6 km. On the uphill stretch she maintains a speed of 12.0 km/h and on the downhill stretch 18.0 km/h. What is her average speed for the entire course?

We will use Eq.(3) derived on Page 3,

vav = (t1 / t) v1 + (t2 / t) v2.(3)

The times for the uphill and downhill stretches, t1 and t2, respectively, are

t1 = s1/v1 = 2/12 = 1/6 h(8)

and

t2 = s2/v2 = 6/18 = 2/6 h(9)

and a total time t equal to

t = 1/6 + 2/6 = 3/6 = 1/2 h. (10)

Substituting Values (8) to (10) into Eq.(3) gives

vav = (1/6)/(1/2) × 12 + (2/6)/(1/2) × 18 = 16 km/h.(11)
