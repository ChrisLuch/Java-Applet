In Series and In Parallel

Answer to the Original Question. If V1 and V2 denote the potential differences across the 3.6-W resistor and the 2.4-W parallel combination, respectively, we must have

V1/V2 = 3.6/2.4 = 1.5 .

We also have

V1 + V2 = 12 V.

These are two equations for the two unknowns V1 and V2. They have the solutions

V1 = 7.2 V and V2 = 4.8 V .

These values agree with the readings of the two voltmeters.

Exercise. Calculate the currents, as shown by the ammeters, in the various branches of the circuit.

Final Exercise. Change the values of the resistances, and calculate the currents and potential differences as shown by the ammeters and voltmeters.
