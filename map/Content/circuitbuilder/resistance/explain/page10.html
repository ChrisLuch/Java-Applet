Resistors In Parallel

Answer. The resistance of the parallel combination of pipes or bulbs is R/2 if the resistance of each pipe or bulb alone is R.

The reason is this. Let us look at the fluid flow situation first. Since both pipes are identical and have the same elevation change, there will be the same current in both pipes. Thus, the total current in both pipes together will be 2I if the current in one pipe is I. Thus, the ratio (gravitational potential difference)/(total current) calculated for the parallel combination of pipes is half that for an individual pipe because the denominator is equal to 2I rather than just I.

The electrical case is analogous. By Kirchhoff's junction rule (current law), the total current in the circuit is equal to the sum of the currents in the two bulbs. Thus, if the current in one bulb is 0.6 A, the total current for two bulbs is 1.2 A. (Compare the ammeters in the top and bottom circuits.) Twice the current in the denominator implies half the resistance, given a constant potential difference (equal to 6 V in the present case).
