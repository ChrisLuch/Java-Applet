Resistors In Parallel

The two sloping pipes and the two light bulbs in the two circuits at the bottom are said to be connected in parallel.

Question. If both pipes and both bulbs are identical and each pipe or bulb has a resistance R, what is the resistance of the parallel combination of pipes or bulbs?

Hint. The water obviously moves through the same change in elevation in both pipes. Similarly, there is the same potential difference of 6 V across each bulb when they are connected in parallel. The reason is that the potentials at the top ends of the two bulbs are equal because the wires connecting the tops have negligible resistance so that the potential difference along these wires is negligible as well. (Potential difference = resistance times current.) For the same reason, the bottom ends of the bulbs are at the same potential.
