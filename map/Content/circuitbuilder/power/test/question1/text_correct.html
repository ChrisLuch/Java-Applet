Yes, the resistor dissipates 24 W of power.

Plan It. We know the potential difference DV across the resistor and the resistance R of the resistor. There is a standard equation that expresses the power P dissipated in a resistor in terms of these two quantities.

Set It Up and Solve It. The equation for the power dissipated in a resistor gives

P = (DV)2/R = 122/6 = 24 W.

Think About It. This means that every second 24 J of electric energy are converted into thermal energy in the resistor. This energy is supplied by the battery where chemical energy is continually being converted to electrical energy while there is a current in the battery.
