Power in Electric Circuits

There is another way of seeing that the top circuit dissipates twice as much power.

The in-series resistors in the bottom circuit are a voltage divider, dividing the total potential difference V0, which is equal to 12 V here, into two equal halves. Thus, in the present case, the potential difference across each one of the resistors in the bottom circuit is 12/2 = 6 V, or V0/2 in general.

Thus, the power dissipated in each of the resistors in the bottom circuit is

P = (V0/2)2/R = (1/4) V02/R = (1/4) Ptop.

The total power dissipated in the bottom circuit is twice this amount because there are two resistors, but even that is only half the power dissipated in the top circuit.
