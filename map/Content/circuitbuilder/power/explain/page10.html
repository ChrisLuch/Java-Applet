Power in Electric Circuits

The powers dissipated in the two circuits are

Ptop = V02/R

and

Pbottom = V02/Rbot .

Substituting Rbot = 1.5 R into the expression for Pbottom gives

Pbottom = V02/(1.5R) = (1/1.5)Ptop

or

Ptop = 1.5 Pbottom.

Task. Check this result numerically, using the present value of 12 V for the battery emf.

Solution. The current in the top circuit is 6 A. The definition of resistance then gives for the resistance R the value 12/6 = 2 W.

In the bottom circuit, the 12-V potential difference across the combination of all three resistors is divided into 8 V across the single horizontal resistor and 4 V across the two in-parallel resistors, in proportion to the resistances R and R/2 of these circuit elements. Then the power dissipated in the horizontal resistor is 82/2 = 32 W and in the two in-parallel resistors together it is 2 x 42/2 = 16 W. This is a a total of 48 W of power dissipated in the bottom circuit.

In the top circuit, 122/2 = 72 W are being dissipated. This is indeed 1.5 times the 48 W dissipated in the bottom circuit.
