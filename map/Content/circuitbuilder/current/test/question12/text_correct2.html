Electric Current

Again, because of charge conservation, the battery cannot create or destroy charge. The role of the battery is to keep charge moving through the bulb and supply the energy that gets dissipated in the bulb in the form of heat and light. How must the battery be connected to be able to do this?

After leaving the bulb at C, the charge enters the battery at C, from there moves inside the battery to A and then through the wire back to B. Thus, electric charge moves in a closed loop, from A to B to C and back to A. Such a closed loop is called an electric circuit.
