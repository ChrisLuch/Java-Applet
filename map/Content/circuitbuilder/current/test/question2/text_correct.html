Yes, Bulb A will burn brighter than Bulb B.

Solution

Plan It. The power dissipated in a bulb, which determines the bulb's brightness, is proportional to the square of the current in the bulb. Thus we need to determine which circuit has the greater current.

Set It Up and Solve It. Given the potential difference across a resistor, the current in the resistor is inversely proportional to the resistance.

Let the resistance of one of the light bulbs be R. Then the resistance of the in-series combination of bulbs in the bottom circuit is 2R. Therefore, the current in the bottom circuit is half as great as the current in the top circuit so that the bulbs in the bottom circuit don't burn as bright as the bulb in the top circuit.

Think About It. Households should be wired so that lamps are not connected in series. Otherwise, when two lamps are turned on they don't burn as bright as a single lamp. Also, when one lamp is turned off, the other one would automatically go out as well.
