Electric Current

Arrangements (a) and (b) will not make the bulb burn.

The reason is that, in neither arrangement, the filament is part of a closed loop. In both arrangements, one end of the filament is connected to one of the battery terminals, while the other end is not connected to anything.

Note that there is a closed loop in arrangement (b) and that the path through the battery forms one segment of this loop, but that the filament is not in the loop. The closed loop in this case is from one battery terminal through the wire to the other battery terminal and then back inside the battery to the first terminal. This is, in fact, a short circuit, which will contain a very large current which in all likelihood will melt the wire, but not light the bulb.
