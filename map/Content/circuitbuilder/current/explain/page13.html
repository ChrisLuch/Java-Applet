Electric Current

Explanation. Since fluid mass (electric charge) is conserved, the fluid mass (charge) going through meters 2 and 3 into the junction at E2 every second is equal to the total mass (charge) flowing out of the junction at E2 towards meter 1. Thus, the mass (charge) per second moving through meter 1 is equal to the sum of the masses (charges) moving through meters 2 and 3 per second.

Generalizing this we get Kirchhoff's junction (node) rule, which is one of the two basic rules governing DC Circuits:

Kirchhoff's Junction (Node) Rule

The sum of the currents going into a junction (node) is equal to the sum of the currents leaving the junction.

The rule is a consequence of charge conservation.
