Electric Current

Explanation of why all bulbs are equally bright. The 6-V batteries in the two circuits raise the charge (fluid) through equal potential differences (elevation changes). Thus, points F, F2 and F3 are at the same high potential (elevation) and points E, E2 and E3 are at the same low potential (elevation).

In the top circuit, a sloping pipe of length a (bulb) connects points E and F while in the bottom circuit we have two parallel pipes of the same length and slope as in the top circuit connecting the corresponding points. Since all pipes have the same slope, the current in each will be the same.
