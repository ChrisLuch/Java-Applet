The circuit now contains an ammeter.  The same current is in both the resistor and the ammeter.  So, by measuring the current in the ammeter, one can measure the current in the resistor.

Voltmeters

Now try to add a voltmeter, following the steps given below, so that it measures the potential difference across the resistor.

  1. Click on the Voltmeter Tool (circuit symbol:  ) .
  2. Click in a space between two grid points below the 10-W resistor where you want to insert the voltmeter. The meter will be inserted there.
  3. Click on the Wire Tool (circuit symbol:  ). Then by dragging with the mouse from one grid point to the next, in a horizontal or vertical direction, connect the voltmeter across the 10-W resistor.

To change any of your wiring, click on the Erase Tool  and then on the item you want to erase, e.g., a wire segment or a meter.

If you have inserted the voltmeter correctly, it will show a voltage of 12 V.  The correct configuration for the voltmeter will be shown on the next page, Page 10.
