Potentials

The electric potential has different values at different points in the circuit.  You can display its value at a given grid point by selecting the Select Tool and then clicking on the grid point.

The program is set so that the potential at the top left corner of the circuit is zero.  This choice is arbitrary.  You can change the potential at any point, by clicking on that point and entering a new value in the data field.  This will automatically change the potentials at all grid points so that for any two given points the difference between their potentials does not change.

The potential at a given point can be chosen arbitrarily, but the difference between the potentials at two points cannot. The latter is an experimentally measurable quantity determined by the physical conditions in the circuit.

For example, the potential difference across the resistor should be -12 V in the present case, going from right to left.  Thus, if you set the potential just to the right of the resistor equal to 4 V, then it should become -8 V just to the left of the resistor.  This is assuming that the positive pole of the battery is on the right. Try it.
