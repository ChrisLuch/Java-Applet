Electric Circuits

Arrangements (a) and (b) will not make the bulb burn.

The reason is that in neither arrangement is the filament a segment in a closed loop. In both arrangements, one end of the filament is connected to one of the battery terminals, while the other end is not connected to anything.

Note that there is a closed loop in arrangement (b). The path through the battery forms one segment of this loop, but the filament is not a segment in the loop. The closed loop in this case is from the left battery terminal through the wire to the right terminal and then back inside the battery. This is, in fact, a short circuit, which will contain a very large current that in all likelihood will melt the wire and get the battery hot, but not light the bulb.
