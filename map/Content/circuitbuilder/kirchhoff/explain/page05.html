Kirchhoff's Laws

We have established that going clockwise around the the loop from A to B to C and through the battery back to A, we encounter the following potential changes.

A to B: VB - VA = -R1I1

B to C: VC - VB = R2I2

C to A: VA - VC = 12 V

Question. Can you write down the equation implied by Kirchhoff's Voltage Law for this loop, using these voltage expressions?

Answer. Kirchhoff's Voltage Law says that the sum of the potential changes around a closed loop is equal to zero. Thus, the sum of the three terms above must be zero,

-R1I1 + R2I2 + 12 V = 0.

We need a second loop equation. It can be obtained by applying the Voltage Law to the loop from B to C and back to B, say, in a clockwise sense. (You could also get a valid equation by going in the opposite sense. This equation would differ by an overall minus sign from the other equation.) Try to write down this equation.
