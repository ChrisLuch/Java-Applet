Kirchhoff's Laws

For the given R-values, the equations

R3I3 + R2I2 = 0.

-R1I1 + R2I2 + 12 V = 0

I1 + I2 = I3

have the solutions

I1 = 2 A

I2 = -1.2 A

I3 = 0.8 A .

You can verify these values by substituting them into the three equations, using the resistance values indicated in the circuit. The negative value for I2 means that the current in resistor R2 is opposite to the chosen positive direction, and therefore downward as it is in resistor R3. Branches 2 and 3 are in-parallel, and currents in parallel branches are always in the same direction.

The calculation demonstrates that you don't need to know the directions of the currents at the beginning. The solution of the problem will tell you what they are!

You may want to solve the three equations above for general resistance values. You should obtain the following answers.

The symbol V0 will denote the terminal voltage of the battery and the abbreviation D the combination

D = R1R2 +R1R3 + R2R3 .

Then

I1 = V0 (R2 + R3)/D

I2 = -V0 R3/D

I3 = V0 R2/D .

The currents could have been calculated more easily by breaking down the circuit into a combination of resistors that are in-series or in-parallel, as is done in Electric Circuits/Resistance/Explain It, Pages 12-14. In fact, the identical circuit is analyzed there. However, not all combinations of resistors can be decomposed in this fashion. So you need to know an approach that works in all cases.
