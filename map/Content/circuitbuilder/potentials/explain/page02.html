Distinguish between the potential at a point and the potential difference between two points. E.g., the potential difference between the points at the bottom and top of the narrow pipe, in the direction of the fluid flow, is

Note that the fluid current in the pipes is from high to low gravitational potential.

Definition of electric potential

Electric potential is defined in analogy to gravitational potential as the electric potential energy of a charged particle of charge q divided by the charge q. The SI-unit of the electric potential is the volt (V).

Since electric current consists of moving charges, the role of electric potential in electric circuits is therefore analogous to that of gravitational potential (essentially elevation) in fluid circuits.
