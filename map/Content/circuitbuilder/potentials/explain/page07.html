Electric Potentials and Potential Differences

The ammeter shows that the current in the meter is equal to 1.2 A.  This is also the current in the resistor. Multiplying the current by the resistor's resistance of 10 W gives

R I  =  10 x 1.2  =  12 V.

In general, the product RI is equal to the magnitude |DV| of the potential change across the resistor,

|DV|= R I,

where R is the resistance of the resistor and I is the current in the resistor. This equation is an immediate consequence of the definition of resistance. See Resistance under Get Information.
