Electric Potentials and Potential Differences

In the following it is assumed that you have worked through Electric Circuits/Basic Facts/Simulate It/Demo and are familiar with the DC Circuit simulation.

Select the Select Tool () and then click at a grid point in the circuit.  The electric potential at that point will be displayed.

Verify that the potential is 12 V anywhere between the positive pole of the battery and the resistor and that it is 0 V anywhere between the negative pole of the battery and the resistor.

You may wonder how the potential can be 0 at the negative pole of the battery, why it isn't negative there. When referring to a battery pole as 'negative', all one means is that this pole is more negative (than the other pole).

One can always add a constant to the potential at a given point, as long as one adds the same constant at all other points. E.g., if one adds -17 V at all points, the potential would be -17 V at the 'negative' pole of the battery, and it would be -5 V at the 'positive' pole. This would not change the difference between the potentials at any given two points.
