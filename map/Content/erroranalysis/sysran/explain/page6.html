Analyzing The Counted Times

Most of your times were probably fairly close to the target time, but there was definitely a "spread" in the data. This is expected -- you aren't going to be able to count the same (or the correct) time every time.

The amount of "spread" in the data is related to how much random error there is. As you can probably tell, this type of uncertainty has no real pattern to it, other than that it's "clumped" around some central point ... which, hopefully, is fairly close to the target time.

One measure of how much spread there is in a set of data is the standard deviation (SD). Remember, about two-thirds of all data points fall between 1 SD below the mean and 1 SD above the mean.
