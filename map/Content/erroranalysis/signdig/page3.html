How many digits to keep?

We found the area of the Object to be 0.2242 ± 0.02390... cm2. However, there's a question as to how many digits we should keep, since we certainly can't keep them all.

Since the uncertainty figure is nowhere near exact -- remember, the choice of the absolute uncertainty in L and W were somewhat arbitrary -- we should round that off.

As far as the Object's area goes, there's a general convention that dictates how many digits we keep. If the first nonzero digit is small (a 0 or a 1), two digits are kept. If that digit isn't small, one digit is kept. In our area example, the first nonzero digit is a 2, so we round the uncertainty to 0.02. (If we happened to find the error was 0.012390..., we would probably keep the first two digits, making the error 0.012.)

So now we have "the area of the Object is 0.2242 ± 0.02 cm2". But then that makes the thousandths and ten-thousandths decimal places of the area worthless -- how can we know them if there's an uncertainty of two hundredths? It makes sense, then, that the last digit of the error determines the last digit of the quantity being measured or calculated.

Finally, now, we can say the area of the Object is 0.22 ± 0.02 cm2. (If that seems like a lot of uncertainty, it is -- remember, there was a huge relative uncertainty on the Object's width, which dominates the error of area.)
