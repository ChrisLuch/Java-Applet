Absolute Error

On the left is an Object, and a ruler to measure the Object's length. Your job is to make the measurement -- that is, come up with a best estimate of the length and attach some sort of uncertainty estimate to it.

When making the uncertainty estimate, try to pick a value such that the range

measurement value

= best estimate ± uncertainty

might encompass about two-thirds of all such measurements made.

For example, if your best estimate is L and if you think that out of 100 measurements of the Object, 70 might be in the range from L - 0.1 cm to L + 0.1 cm, then put 0.1 cm as your estimate for the uncertainty DL.
