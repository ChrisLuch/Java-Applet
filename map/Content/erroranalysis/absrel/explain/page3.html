Relative Error

On the left is the same object and ruler once more. This time, though, the absolute uncertainty is given.

Your job is to enter a best estimate of the length and calculate the relative uncertainty (relative error) -- that is, to compare the size of the error to the measurement itself, and express it as a percentage.

The relative uncertainty (relative error) is defined as the fraction

rel. err. = (abs. err.) / (best estimate) = DL / L.

For example, if the absolute error is 2.5 m and the best estimate is 20.0 m, the relative error is

relative error = 2.5 m / 20.0 m = 0.125 .

Note that a relative error is always a pure number. It is customary to express a relative error as percentage error. To convert to a percentage, multiply the fraction representing the error by 100. Thus, in the present example,

percentage error = 0.125×100 = 12.5% .
