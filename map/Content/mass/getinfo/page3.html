Power in Mechanical Systems

One-dimensional motion

Let the motion be along the x-axis. A force with x-component Fx is acting on a particle with x-component of velocity vx. Then the "power applied" to the particle, i.e., the rate at which the force is doing work on the particle (transmitting energy to the particle is equal to

P = Fxvx .

Two-dimensional motion

In this case, the expression above is replaced by the scalar product in two dimensions,

P = Fxvx + Fyvy = Fv cos q,

where F and v are the magnitude of the force and the magnitude of the particle's velocity, respectively, and q is the angle between the force and velocity vectors.

Three-dimensional motion

P = Fxvx + Fyvy + Fyvz = Fv cos q.
