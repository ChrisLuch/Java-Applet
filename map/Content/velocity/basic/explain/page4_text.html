Velocity

Select the Pos(ition) Path check box to show the car's path (in blue), and then move the car with varying speed along some curved path. Stop the motion, and click the REPLAY button. This will reset the car to its start position, but will not erase the car's path. Now unselect the Car check box, and then click the PLAY button. You will see the velocity vector move along the car's previous path without the car being visible. View the motion both in velocity space (Velocity Space selected) or position space (Velocity Space selected).

Observe that the velocity vector is tangential to the path, at all times, and that its magnitude is always proportional to the speed with which the vector (actually, the car) is moving along the path.

You can slow down the motion by stepping through it, rather than playing it continuously. To do so, click the STEP button rather than the PLAY button. Stepping through the motion will also make it easier to observe that the distance travelled by the car in one step (equal time interval) is proportional to the magnitude of the velocity vector.
