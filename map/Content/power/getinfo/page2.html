Power

Whether energy is generated or dissipated in a process, the instantaneous power P of the process is defined by

P = dE/dt,

where dE is the infinitesimal amount of energy generated or dissipated at the given instant during the infinitesimal time interval dt.

The average power, Pav, generated or dissipated during a finite time interval Dt is defined as

Pav = DE/Dt

where DE is the energy generated or dissipated during the time interval Dt.

The SI-unit of power is the watt (W),

1 W = 1 J s-1.

A non-SI unit of power is the horsepower (hp). 1 hp = 745.7 W.
