Definition

The instantaneous power, P, is the rate in time at which energy is generated or dissipated at a given instant. For details, see Page 2.

Note about energy generation or dissipation

Energy can be neither created nor destroyed. When talking about energy 'generation' or 'dissipation' we are talking about processes of energy transformation. E.g., in an electrical power plant, chemical energy is transformed into electrical energy (and thermal energy) and one refers to this as 'generation' of electrical energy.

Dissipation of energy takes place in processes involving mechanical friction or the passage of electrical charge through resistors. In either case, ordered mechanical or electrical energy is transformed into less ordered thermal energy.
