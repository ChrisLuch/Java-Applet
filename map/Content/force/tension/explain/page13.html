Tension in a Dynamic Situation

Observe the tensions in the three ropes and the masses of the three blocks. Can you detect a systematic relationship between the tensions and the masses?

Let us again take the masses of the three blocks, from front to back, as 50 kg, 30 kg, and 20 kg, respectively, and the applied force as 60 N.

Then the tension in the last string is 12 N. This is a fraction of the 60-N force applied to the system equal to 12/60 = 0.2. This fraction is equal also to the ratio (mass of last block)/(total mass) = 20/100 = 0.2.

Similarly, the tension in the middle string is 30 N which is a fraction of 30/60 = 0.5 of the 60-N applied force. The combined mass of the two blocks pulled by the middle string is the same fraction of the total mass, namely, (20+30)/100 = 0.5.

Do you think the tension and mass fractions at a given point in the system are equal no matter what the masses of the blocks and the applied force are? If so, this would provide an easy means of working out the tensions at different points in a system of this kind.
