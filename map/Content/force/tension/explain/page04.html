Tension - What Is It?

The tension at a given point in a rope is equal to the magnitude of the force that one portion of the rope exerts on the other portion at this point. The more tension there is, the harder the two portions are pulling on each other.

Question: Is tension a vector or a scalar quantity?

Answer: Tension is defined as the magnitude of a force. Magnitudes are scalars. Thus, tension is a scalar also. Tension does not have direction. What does have direction is the force that one portion of a rope exerts on the adjoining portion. Tension is only the magnitude of this force.
