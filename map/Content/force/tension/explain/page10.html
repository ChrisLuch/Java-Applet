Variable Tension

The weight of the rope is set to 30 N. The tension T increases as one goes up the rope, from 0 at the bottom to 30 N at the top. At the point shown here, the tension is 9.0 N. Let's call this point P. The tension at P is equal to the weight w of the portion of the rope below P.

Reason: The subsidiary diagram shows the entire portion of the rope below point P. The magnitude of the upward force F acting at P on this portion of the rope is equal to the tension at P. Since the rope is at rest, F must balance w, by Newton's second law. Therefore the tension at P is equal to w.

How large is w? If the length of the portion below P is y and the total length of the rope is L, then w is equal to the fraction y/L of the total weight W of the rope. Thus,

T = w = (y/L)W .

With T = 9.0 N and W = 30 N, this tells us that

y/L = 9/30 = 0.3

so that the point at which the tension is 9.0 N is 30% up from the bottom of the rope.
