Tension in a Dynamic Situation

Let us calculate the three tensions when the blocks' masses, from right to left, are m1 = 50 kg, m2 = 30 kg, and m3 = 20 kg, respectively, and the applied force F = 60 N.

We will need the magnitude a of the acceleration. Newton's second law applied to the entire system gives the equation

F = 60 N = (m1+m2+m3)a = (20+30+50)a = 100 a
whence
a = 60/100 = 0.60 m s-2 .

The only horizontal force acting on the last block in the chain is the force exerted by the string attached to it. The magnitude of this force is equal to the tension T3 in the string. Applying Newton's second law to this block therefore gives the equation

T3 = m3a = 20x0.6 = 12 N.

To obtain an equation for the tension T2 in the middle rope, we apply Newton's second law to the middle block of mass m2 = 30 kg. The third rope exerts a backward force of magnitude T3 on this block and the second rope a forward force of magnitude T2. Thus,

T2 - T3 = m2a = 30x0.6 = 18 N
whence
T2 = 18 + T3 = 18 + 12 = 30 N .
