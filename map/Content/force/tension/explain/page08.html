Tension - What Is It?

The subsidiary diagram introduces the following notation. The points where forces F4 and F5 are acting are labeled I and II, respectively. We wish to find the tension at point P, somewhere between points I and II. The segment of rope that extends from point P to the right end of the rope is labeled segment A. Its outline is drawn with a continuous line. To find the tension at P, we need to know the force exerted on segment A at point P by the main portion of the rope, to the left of point P. This portion is denoted segment B and drawn with a stippled outline.

Only the forces acting on segment A are shown. They are the two external forces labeled F5 and F6 and the internal force, labeled Fint, which is the force exerted on segment A by segment B at point P. (Force F4 is acting on segment B, not A.)

As the simulation indicates, the tension at point P is 900 N. To find the tension in this segment, we need to apply Newton's second law to segment A. The reasoning is like that on Page 3.

Since segment A is at rest and therefore has zero acceleration, the net force acting on this segment must equal zero, by Newton's second law. Since F5 + F6 = 600 + 300 = 900 N, Fint must have a magnitude of 900 N, to balance the other two forces. By definition of tension, the tension at P is equal to Fint and therefore equal to 900 N.
