Acceleration in Fletcher's Trolley

Step 3. Write down the equations resulting when Newton's second law is applied to all bodies for which fbd's have been drawn. Also, write down any other equations that are necessary to solve the problem and explain why these equations can be applied to the given situation.

Newton 2 applied to block 1, x-direction only, when the blocks are in motion:

T1 = m1a.(1)

where a is the x-component of acceleration of block 1.

Newton 2 applied to block 2, y-direction only, when the blocks are in motion:

W2 - T2 = m2a.(2)

Since the string is inextensible, the y-component of acceleration of block 2 is equal to the x-component of acceleration of block 1. So the same symbol a can be used for both in equations (1) and (2).

Equations (1) and (2) involve three unknowns: the two tensions T1 and T2 and the acceleration a. We need a third equation to solve for the three unknowns. What do you think will be the third equation?
