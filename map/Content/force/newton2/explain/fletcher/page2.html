Acceleration in Fletcher's Trolley

Answer. The magnitude of the acceleration of the two blocks is 3.92 m s-2, assuming the masses of blocks 1 and 2 are 0.6 kg and 0.4 kg, respectively, and the acceleration due to gravity is taken to have the magnitude g = 9.8 m s-2.

To calculate this value, we follow our standard four-step procedure for problems involving Newton's second law. (Newton's second law is involved because the given situation involves forces and accelerations.)

Step 1. Make free-body diagrams for all relevant objects, which are blocks 1 and 2 in this case. Click the fbd toggle button, fifth button from the left, to display the fbd's for the two blocks. Step through the motion using the second button from the left. Notice that the tensions T1 and T2 in the strings where the strings are attached to the two blocks have one value when the blocks are held at rest and a smaller value when the blocks are in motion (animation paused).

Step 2. Draw axes and define their positive directions. The axes that will be used here are displayed also when the fbd toggle button is pressed. The x-axis is horizontal and to the right and the y-axis vertical and downward.
