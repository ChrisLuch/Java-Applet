Tension in Fletcher's Trolley

We want to solve the two equations

T = m1a.(1)

and

W2 - T = m2a(2)

for the tension T. We have already solved for the acceleration and found

a = [m2/(m1 + m2)] g.(5)

Substituting this expression into equation (1) gives

T = [m1m2/(m1 + m2)] g.(7)

Substituting the given values gives for the tension when the blocks are in motion

T = [0.6x0.4/(0.6 + 0.4)] x 9.8

= 2.4 N.(8)

Note that this is less than the weight W2 = 0.4x9.8 = 3.9 N. When the blocks are in motion, the string tension is less than the weight of block 2. This must be so if block 2 is to accelerate downward. In order for block 2 to accelerate downward, the net force on block 2 must be downward as well. This will only be the case if the weight of block 2 is greater than the tension!

Note, however, that when block 1 is held at rest, so that block 2 is at rest as well while suspended from the string, the tension in the string must be equal to the weight of block 2, because in this case the net force acting on block 2 is zero. When block 1 is held at rest, there must be a horizontal force acting on block 1 to the left to counteract the tension force acting on block 1. This horizontal force must have a magnitude F equal to the tension in the string, which at this moment is equal to the weight of block 2.

Final comments.

(a) Verify that expressions (5) and (7) above for a and T also satsify equation (2).

(b) Practise calculating the tension and the acceleration for different mass values of block 2. (The mass of block 1 is fixed at 0.6 kg.) Then run the simulation and check your answers.

(c) The simulation also lets you set the pulley mass to a non-zero value (by setting the moment of inertia of the pulley unequal to zero). In that case the tensions on the two sides of the pulley are no longer equal. If you are interested in seeing how to calculate the two tensions and the acceleration in that case, go to Torque/About an Axis/Apply It/Fletcher.
