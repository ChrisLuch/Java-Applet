Question. If you stand in an elevator on a bathroom scale while the elevator has an upward acceleration of 2.0 m s-2, what weight will the scale show? Assume your mass to be 60 kg.

The applet on the left can simulate this situation. The table is like the floor of an elevator, the springs represent the mechanism of a bathroom scale, and the 2.0-kg object takes the place of a person standing on the scale.

Observe the changes in the system when you select an upward 2.0-m s-2 acceleration (drag the orange dot upward or move the acceleration slider until it shows 2.0 m s-2). The blue arrow on the left represents the normal force exerted by the springs (bathroom scale) on the 2.0-kg object. Can you predict the magnitude N of this force?
