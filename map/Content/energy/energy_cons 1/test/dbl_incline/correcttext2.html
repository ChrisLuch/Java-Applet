What is the Acceleration?

Strategy

Let the system consist of the blocks, pulley, string, incline, and earth.

The system has no friction, so its mechanical energy is conserved. The mechanical energy consists of the kinetic energies of the two blocks and the gravitional potential energies of the block 1/earth and block 2/earth pairs. (The pulley's mass and, therefore, its kinetic energy are zero.)

Energy conservation leads to an equation relating the change of the square of the blocks' speed and the displacement. This equation is a standard one from kinematics containing the acceleration.
