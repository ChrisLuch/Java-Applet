What is the Acceleration?

We have now derived the following expression for az:

az = -[ (m1/M) sin a - (m2/M) sin b ] g.

Substituting the given values gives, since M = 0.6 + 0.4 = 1.0 kg,

az = -[0.6 sin 30o - 0.4 sin 60o] = 0.45 m s-2.

Since az is positive, the left block accelerates up the incline.

Thinking about the answer

The dimensions of the expression for az should be those of acceleration. That is obviouslious the case because of the factor of g. The ratios of the masses and the sine functions are dimensionless.

The signs make sense as well. The term involving m1 should cause the acceleration to be negative. It does that. Similarly the term involving m2 should cause the acceleration to be positive, which it does.

You can also check some limiting cases. E.g., if m1 = 0, a = 0, and b = 90o, the acceleration should be equal to g. The equation for az gives this answer.

You can now experiment with the simulation for arbitrary angles of the inclines and masses of the blocks, observe the acceleration, and check the equation above against the observed values.
