What is the Acceleration?

Solving the equations

Dv2 = -2 [ (m1/M) g Dh1 + (m1/M) g Dh1 ].

Dv2 = 2 az Dz .

Substituting the second expression for Dv2 into the equation at the top and dividing by 2 gives

az Dz = (m1/M) g Dh1 + (m2/M) g Dh2.

Underneath, place the three gifs from the Explain It part that include the two triangles defining the elevation changes in terms of Dz. Then continue underneath that with the following text:

The triangles above show that

Dh1 = (sin a) Dz,

Dh2 = -(sin b) Dz.

There has to be a minus sign in the second equation because h2 decreases as z increases. Notice the opposite sense of the arrows with Dh2 and Dz in the second triangle.

Substituting these expressions into the equation for az and dividing by Dz gives

az = -[ (m1/M) sin a - (m2/M) sin b ] g.
