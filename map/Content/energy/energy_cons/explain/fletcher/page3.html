Energy Conservation in Fletcher's Trolley

Since both blocks are at rest initially, the initial energy of the system is

Ein = KE1,in + KE2,in + PEin,

= 0 + 0 + m2ghin

and the final energy when both blocks have the same final speed v is

Efin = KE1,fin + KE2,fin + PEfin,

= [(m1 + m2)/2]v2 + m2ghfin.

Equating the two energies and isolating the term with v gives

[(m1 + m2)/2]v2 = m2g(hin - hfin)

whence

v2 = 2[m2/(m1 + m2)]g(hin - hfin).

In the case where hfin = 0, this reduces to

v2 = 2[m2/(m1 + m2)]ghin.

Substituting the given values, this gives

v2 = 2[0.4/(0.6 + 0.4)]x9.8x2.5

= 19.6 (m/s)2.

Taking the square root gives for the speed when block 2 reaches h = 0 the value

v = 4.4 m/s.
