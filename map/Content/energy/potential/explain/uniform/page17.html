Potential Energy For A Block On An Incline

Answer. We can calculate the block's final speed if we know its final kinetic energy, which can be calculated by using the work-energy theorem. The amounts of work Wa done by the applied force and Wfr done by the friction force are equal to

Wa = 5.81 J

and

Wfr = -1.62 J .

The calculations are done on Page 18. The work done by the normal force is zero.

The vertical elevation change is given by the y-component of the block's displacement, dy = d sin q = 2 x sin 200 = 0.68 m. Therefore, the change in the block's potential energy equals

DPE = mgdy = 0.4x10x0.68

= 2.74 J

The work-kinetic energy theorem gives

Wa + Wfr = DKE + DPE

whence

DKE = Wa + Wfr - DPE

= 1.45 J .

Therefore,

KEfin = KEin + DKE

= (m/2)vin2 + DKE

= (0.4/2)x22 + 1.45

= 2.25 J .

From this we can obtain the final speed as

vfin = [2KEfin/m]1/2

= [2x2.25/0.4]1/2

= 3.35 m/s .

You can check this value by running the simulation with the given data.
