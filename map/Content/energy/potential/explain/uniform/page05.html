The Concept of Potential Energy

Summary of Qualitative Features of Potential Energy

  • Potential energy is a quantity that applies to two-body systems, not individual bodies. When using the phrase potential energy of a ball, one is using simplified language, used only for brevity's sake. The so-called 'potential energy of the ball' is really the potential energy of the ball-earth system.
  • The potential energy of two gravitationally interacting objects depends on the distance between the two objects. It increases as the distance increases.
  • If two objects are moving subject to only their gravitational interaction, the sum of the potential and kinetic energies of this system is conserved, in other words, it does not change during the motion.

Comment. The force of gravity is called conservative because a potential energy can be associated with it, such that the sum of kinetic and potential energy is constant (energy conservation) if no other forces are acting on the system. There are also non-conservative forces, with which no potential energy can be associated. Examples of the latter occur in the systems studied on Pages 13 to 18 in this session.
