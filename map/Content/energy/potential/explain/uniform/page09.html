Potential Energy Quantitative

Question. What is the ball's gravitational potential energy at y = 6 m? Take the ball's mass to be m = 0.4 kg, and the magnitude of the acceleration due to gravity as g = 10 m/s2 here, so that you can calculate in your head.

Answer. The equation derived on Page 8 gives

PE = mgy = 0.4x10x6 = 24 J.

Question. Suppose the ball's kinetic energy at y = 6 m is 30 J and that the ball is moving upward at this point. The ball then reaches the top and falls back down. As it passes the point at y = 10 m on the way down, what is its kinetic energy?

Answer. As the ball moves from y = 6 m to the top of its flight path and then back down to y = 10 m, the change in its kinetic energy is the negative of its change in potential energy. (The fact that the ball first rises and then falls does not matter.) Thus,

KE10 - KE6 = - (PE10 - PE6) .

With PE10 = 0.4x10x10 = 40 J and PE6 = 24 J, this gives

KE10 - 30 = - (40 - 24) = -16 J

whence

KE10 = 30 - 16 = 14 J.

You can check these values with the simulation, roughly. Make sure the E and y scales have their zeros on the same level, so that PE = mgy.
