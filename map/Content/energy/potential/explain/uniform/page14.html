Potential Energy in Pendulum Motion

Now let's use energy conservation to study the simple pendulum quantitatively. Our pendulum consists of a massive bob attached to a massless rigid rod. You can drag the bob to the starting position you want. The pendulum has no friction.

Run the simulation. Button 1 will display the two external forces acting on the pendulum, the gravitational force exerted on the bob by the earth and the force of constraint exerted on the pendulum rod by the supporting bearings.

The force of constraint exerted by the bearings on the pendulum does no work on the pendulum if the bearings are frictionless. Thus, no energy loss at the bearings enters the energy balance and the sum of gravitational potential energy and kinetic energy of the pendulum is conserved. However, even though the constraint force has no bearing on the energy balance, it has a significant effect on the motion: without it, the pendulum bob would not move in a circular arc.

The constraint force acting on the pendulum is an example of a non-conservative force. See the comment at the bottom of Page 5. Non-conservative forces are forces with which no potential energy is associated.
