Potential Energy Quantitative

So far, we have considered only vertical motions. The potential energy concept and the expression mgy + const for the gravitational potential energy applies also to projectile motions in which the ball's velocity has a non-zero horizontal component.

The gravitational potential energy of an object of mass m near the earth's surface is given by

PE = mgy + const

no matter what the object's horizontal x-coordinate is. The gravitational potential energy depends only on an object's elevation y because the gravitational force is doing no work on an object moving horizontally.

Question. A 0.4-kg ball that is at y = 0 has a speed v = 10 m/s and, therefore, a kinetic energy KE = 20 J. Take PE = 10 J at y = 0, as set in the simulation. What will be the potential energy and speed of the ball at y = - 2 m?

Work this out for an arbitrary launch angle. The answer should not depend on this angle. Taking PE = 10 J at y = 0 will give you some practice with the 'const' term in the expression for PE.

You may first want to find the answer 'experimentally' with the simulation. Try it for several launch angles. You can set the ball to the right speed by watching the kinetic energy bar. You can drag the ball horizontally to create more room for the motion.
