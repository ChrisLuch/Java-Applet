Potential Energy Quantitative

Answer. When the ball moves upward, its displacement is opposite to the gravitational force. Therefore the gravitational work done on the ball is negative. Since the displacement Dy is positive and the magnitude mg of the gravitational force is positive, we must write

Wgrav = - mgDy.

This equation is also correct when the ball moves downward because in that case Dy < 0 so that the right-hand side is positive overall, which is correct because the gravitational force is in the direction of the displacement in this case.

Comparing the preceding equation with

DPE = - Wgrav
gives
DPE = mgDy .

This equation is satisfied if we take the potential energy as

PE = mgy.

This expression gives PE = 0 at y = 0. We will see on Page 10 that there are other possible expressions for PE that do not have this simple property.
