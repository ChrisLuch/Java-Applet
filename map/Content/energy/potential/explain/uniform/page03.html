The Concept of Potential Energy

Play the animation and observe the motion.

Three Questions.

  1. After the ball has been thrown, how many bodies are involved in making the ball's motion the way it is?
  2. What does the ball's motion have in common with the motion on Page 2?
  3. What is different?

Answers.

  1. Two bodies, the ball and the earth. If the earth were not there, the ball would continue to move upward when thrown upward.
  2. As the distance between the ball and the earth increases (ball rising), the ball slows down and its kinetic energy decreases. As the distance decreases (ball falling), the ball speeds up and its kinetic energy increases.
  3. The earth does not move perceptibly as the ball goes up and down. (This is so because the earth is so much more massive than the ball. However, the earth does move as the ball moves, but only a tiny tiny amount. Thus, in principle, this is a two-body system just like the system on Pages 1 and 2.)
