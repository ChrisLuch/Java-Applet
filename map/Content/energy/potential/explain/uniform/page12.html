Potential Energy Quantitative

Answer. If the potential energy is equal to 10 J at y = 0, then at an arbitrary elevation y

PE = mgy + 10 J .

Therefore, at y = - 2 m, we have

PE-2 = 0.4x10x(-2) + 10 = 2J.

By energy conservation,

KE-2 + PE-2 = KE0 + PE0.

Therefore

KE-2 = KE0 + PE0 - PE-2

whence

KE-2 = 20 + 10 - 2 = 28 J.

This gives for the ball's speed at y = - 2 m the value

v-2 = [2KE/m]1/2 = [2x28/0.4]1/2 = 11.8 m/s.
