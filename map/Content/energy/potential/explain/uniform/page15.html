Potential Energy in Pendulum Motion

Answer. As the bob moves from q = -120o to q = 0o, its elevation decreases by 4[cos 0 - cos (-120o)] = 6 m. At the same time, its gravitational potential energy decreases by mx9.8x6 J where m is the mass of the bob in kg.

Because of energy conservation, the kinetic energy increases by the same amount. Since the kinetic energy is zero at q = -120o, the kinetic energy at the bottom is

KEbot = mx9.8x6.

Taking the kinetic energy to be all translational kinetic energy, we get

(m/2)vbot2 = mx9.8x6.

The mass cancels out of this equation. Thus, the speed at the bottom is independent of the mass and equal to

vbot = [2x9.8x6]1/2 = 10.8 m/s .

You can check this value with the simulation. You will be able to do this only approximately because it is hard to stop the motion exactly at the bottom.
