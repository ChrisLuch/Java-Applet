The Concept of Potential Energy

In the double-star system, both bodies are moving. In contrast, in the ball-earth system, it appears as if only the ball is moving while the earth is motionless. However, appearances can be deceiving. The earth does move a tiny bit in opposite direction to the ball, first down and then back up as the ball goes up and then comes down. The earth's movement is not noticeable because the earth's mass is huge compared to that of the ball.

Question. On Page 1 it is stated that the potential energy of two gravitationally interacting bodies increases as the bodies move apart, and that the kinetic energy decreases by exactly the amount by which the potential energy increases.

Is this true for the ball-earth system as well?

Answer. When the ball is going up, its speed is decreasing, and so is its kinetic energy. This agrees with the statement qualitatively. To check whether it is true quantitatively, we need to measure the potential and kinetic energy changes during the ball's motion. Run the animation again.
