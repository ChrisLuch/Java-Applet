Torque About an Axis

What variables does a torque depend on?

The torque due to a force depends on

  1. the magnitude of the force
  2. the direction of the force
  3. the reference point
  4. the point at which the force is acting.

It has been stated earlier that the torque depends on the magnitude of the force and the lever arm. Items 2 to 4 together determine the lever arm. Let's investigate them separately. You have already experimented with Item 3 on Page 4. Here, let us investigate items 2 and 4.

Draw a single force vector and, keeping the reference point fixed, change either the direction of the force (by dragging its tip) or the point at which the force is acting (by dragging the entire vector at its midpoint).

Observe the resulting motions. You should be able to achieve positive, zero, or negative torques by making either of these changes. Try it.
