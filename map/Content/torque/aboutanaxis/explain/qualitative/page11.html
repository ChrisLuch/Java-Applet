Torque About an Axis

Net torque and net force

If several forces act on the object, each one contributes a torque relative to point O. The sum of these torques is the net torque. If the net torque is zero, the object will not start rotating.

Torques and forces are related, but they are different quantities. It is possible to have two forces acting on an object so that the net force is non-zero while the net torque is zero. Vice versa, it is possible to have two forces acting on the object such that the net force is zero while the corresponding net torque is non-zero. The latter arrangement is called a force couple.
