Torque About an Axis

Magnitude of torque. The torque created by a force is larger if the force is larger. However, the torque is determined not only by the force itself, but also by the lever arm. Given the force and where it is acting, the lever arm, and with it the torque, depends on the location of point O.

Try the following. Make a single force act on the square and drag point O to a new location, which can even be outside the square. Notice how the torque changes as you move point O. Then click the PLAY button.

Observe both the rate of rotation and the sense of rotation, clockwise or counter-clockwise, for different choices of O. The simulation is designed so that the rate of rotation is proportional to the torque, although it is not realistic in other respects.

Important lesson: The torque depends on the location of point O.
