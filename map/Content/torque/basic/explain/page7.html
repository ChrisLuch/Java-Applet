Torque and Rotation

Each force acting on an object has a torque associated with it. The sum of all torques is called the net torque. If the net torque is non-zero, the state of rotation of the object will change. The net torque determines the object's angular acceleration, both its magnitude and sign. The net torque is directly proportional to the angular acceleration, just as the net force acting on the object is directly proportional to the acceleration of the CM.

PLAY with the simulation and explore the relationship between forces, and where they are acting, and the resulting rotational motions. Observe the value of the net torque.

Apply forces that are in directions other than vertical and observe under what conditions the torque is positive or negative and observe the corresponding angular acceleration of the object. Does a positive torque always tend to rotate the object counter-clockwise (positive angular acceleration)?

You can change the object's shape and the location of the CM by clicking on the CM and then dragging it with the mouse. You can move the entire object to another location by dragging the object at a point other than the CM.
