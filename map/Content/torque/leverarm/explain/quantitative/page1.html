Calculating a Lever Arm

PreparationTorque/Lever Arm/Explain It/Qualitative.

Experiment with the simulation until you are sure you know how to find the lever arm. You can vary the force (mustard-colored vector) by dragging its tip, change the point at which the force is acting (red dot at the tail end of the force vector) by dragging the vector, and you can drag point O.

The simulation assumes there is a rotation axis perpendicular to the screen through O. The blue line marks the distance r from the rotation axis to the point at which the force is acting. This line is in general not the lever arm.

To display the lever arm (in green), select LEVER ARM. The lever arm is the perpendicular distance from the rotation axis onto the line along which the force is acting (shown as a dotted line).
