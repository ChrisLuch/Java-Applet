Observing the Motion

To be able to visualize the motion shown by the applet, imagine a spool made of an inner cylinder (grey) rigidly connecting two outer disks (blue). The spool moves so that its inner cylinder is rolling without slipping on a horizontal surface (salmon-colored). A point (black) drawn on the outer disk facing you is revolving with the disk.

Exercise 1.

RESET the applet. Make sure the spool support is set to "Bottom".

Set the three sliders as follows:

  • angular speed (w): 1.00 rad/s
  • center-to-point distance (a): 0.25 m
  • inner radius of spool (r): 0.15 m

The outer radius of the spool, R, is fixed at 0.50 m.

Select the Trace toggle button , and PLAY the motion.

After the spool has moved some distance, click the REVERSE button , then PLAY again, and then PAUSE . You should be able to construct a looped path as in Figure 1 below.

Exercise 2. PLAY or REVERSE the motion. Try to duplicate (approximately) the coordinate values in the data box on the right in Figure 4. The point on the spool would be oriented as in Figure 5 below. All coordinates in the data box on the right are represented in the applet window at a scale of 2 pix = 1 cm.

Figure 5

  • (X,Y)-coordinates. These are the coordinates of the center of the spool. The X-coordinate has its origin at the left edge of the applet window. The X-coordinate of the midpoint of the applet window will depend on the width of the window. The Y-coordinate of the center of the spool is chosen to be 0, whether you are working with the "Bottom" or "Top" setting of the spool support.
  • (x,y)-coordinates. These are the coordinates of the revolving point relative to the Lab frame, which is fixed relative to the applet window. The origin of the (x,y)-coordinates coincides with that of the (X,Y)-coordinates.
  • (x',y')-coordinates. These are the coordinates of the revolving point relative to the frame in which the spool is at rest, i.e., relative to the frame moving along with the spool. The (x',y')-axes are fixed in orientation, however. They are not rotating with the spool. The origin of the (x',y')-coordinates is at the center of the spool.

    The x, x', and X values increase going to the right and the y, y', and Y values going upward.

    Working with the values in the data box on the right in Figure 4 or with those displayed by your applet, verify that the three sets of coordinates satisfy the following addition rules:

    x = x' + X (1a)

    y = y' + Y (1b)

    E.g., using the data from Figure 4, Equation (1a) amounts to

    2.41 m = -0.22 m + 2.63 m (2)

    which adds up correctly.
