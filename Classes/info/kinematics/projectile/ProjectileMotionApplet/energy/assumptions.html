Help - Work and Energy in Projectile Motion

The applet shows a ball performing projectile motion without air resistance and shows the ball's kinetic and potential energies during the motion

The simulation assumes that the ball's motion is subject only to the force of gravity and that this force is uniform throughout the range of the motion.

The coordinate system used consists of a horizontal x-axis pointing to the right and a vertical y-axis pointing upward.

The gravitational potential energy PE of the ball-earth system (briefly: the ball's potential energy) is defined so that PE = 0 when the ball's elevation y is equal to zero.
