You picked choice (c), saying that bulb B will burn less bright than bulb A.

Maybe your reasoning was that there is a total amount of power available that is shared among three bulbs in the bottom circuit, not just among two as in the top circuit. One cannot reason this way because the power dissipated in a circuit depends on the configuration of the circuit.

Hint: In the top circuit, bulb A is in a series with a single bulb, identical to A. In the bottom circuit, bulb B is in series with a parallel combination of two bulbs identical to B (and A).

What is the resistance of these two bulbs in parallel? Is it greater than, equal to, or less than that of a single bulb?
