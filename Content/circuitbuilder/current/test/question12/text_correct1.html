Making A Light Bulb Burn

Correct!

To have the bulb burn, there must be electric current in its filament. Electric current consists of moving electric charge. To have a current in the filament, electric charge must move into the bulb at contact point B and out at contact point C, or the other way around.

The reason why charge that goes into the bulb must again come out of the bulb is that electric charge is conserved. Conservation of electric charge is a fundamental law of nature. If charge were not conserved, charge might go into the bulb at contact B and get 'used up' in lighting the bulb. If that were so, the charge would not come out at contact point C and we would not need to connect point C to the battery.
