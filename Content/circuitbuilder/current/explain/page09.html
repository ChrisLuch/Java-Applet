Electric Current

Explanation: In the fluid flow analogy, a pump (battery) elevates the fluid (charge) from E to F by equal amounts (6-V potential difference) in both circuits.

From point F, the fluid (charge) moves downhill (decreasing potential) through the bulb(s). A single bulb is represented by a narrow pipe of length a. You can see that two pipes of total length 2a (two bulbs in series) will be sloped less than a single pipe of length a if the vertical drop of the pipes is the same in both cases.

Less slope means the fluid will move downhill more slowly which means that the current will be smaller in the bottom circuit, which is what we wanted to explain.

Important note. The fluid current in a given pipe is proportional to the elevation change. Similarly, the electric current in a given bulb is proportional to the electric potential difference across the bulb.
