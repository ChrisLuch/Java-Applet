Electric Current

On the left are shown are two identical circuits with identical bulbs. Bulbs 2 and 3 are connected in parallel, and this parallel combination is connected in series with bulb 1. There are three switches in the circuits. The only difference in the two circuits is in the states of the switches, whether they are open or closed.

Question. When you close the open switch in the top circuit, how will the three bulbs in the top circuit burn compared to those in the bottom circuit? Think about this and then check your answer by closing the switch.

Suggestion: You may want to insert ammeters in both circuits to measure the currents in all bulbs. This requires three meters per circuit. Meters show more clearly than the bulbs' brightness how large the currents are in each bulb.
