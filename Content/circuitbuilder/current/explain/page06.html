Electric Current

The ammeter must be inserted in series with the bulb to measure the current in the bulb. This way the current is the same in both the meter and the bulb because the same amount of charge moves through the meter every second as through the bulb. Remember that charge is conserved (cannot be created or destroyed) and that, in the steady state, it does not accumulate at any point in the cirucuit.

The ammeter shows a current of 0.6 A (ampere; or 'amp' for short). This means that 0.6 coulomb of charge pass through the meter (and the bulb) every second.

Definition

The magnitude of a current at a point in a circuit is defined to be 1 ampere (A) if 1 Coulomb (C) of charge passes that point in 1 second (s),

1 A = 1 C/s.
