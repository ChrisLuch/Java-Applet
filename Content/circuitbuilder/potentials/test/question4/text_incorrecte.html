Incorrect

There are three identical resistors in the circuit. So one might think that each resistor has one third of the total potential difference of 12 V across it, which would be 4 V for each resistor. This would be true if the resistors were all in series. However, they are not.

Hint. The single resistor on the left is in series with the in-parallel combination of two resistors on the right. How is the total 12-V potential difference divided among these two in-series resistance elements?

Remember that each of the three resistors has the same resistance. Let's call it R. To answer the preceding question, you need to work out the combined resistance of the in-parallel combination of resistors in terms of R. (For the necessary equation see Resistance under Get Information.)
