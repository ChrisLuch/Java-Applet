Electric Potentials and Potential Differences

Notice that the direction of the current, outside the battery, is from high potential to low potential.  This is the conventional current direction which is defined to be that in which positive electric charges would move.  For more information on current direction, see 'Current' under Get Information.

Electric potential is analogous to elevation, and electric current is analogous to a current of water.  As water flows from higher to lower elevation, so does positive electric charge move from higher to lower electric potential.

The electric potential changes only in the parts of the circuit where there is resistance.  It does not change significantly along the wires because wires have very small resistance.
