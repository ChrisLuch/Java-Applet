Electric Potentials and Potential Differences

Answer: The electric potential energy PE of a particle of charge q at some point is equal to q times the electric potential V at that point,

PE = qV.

The electric potential energy PE of any charge, positive or negative, decreases in going through a resistor because a resistor heats up when charged particles pass through it and this thermal energy is taken from the electric potential energy of the particles passing through.

If the PE of a charge q decreases as q goes through a resistor, then, if q is positive, q must be moving from high to low potential. Since positive charges move in the conventional current direction, this direction is from high to low potential as well.
