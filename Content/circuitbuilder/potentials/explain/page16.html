Electric Potentials and Potential Differences

The circuit shown on the left is identical to the one on the previous page, but with the two meters removed. The switch is still open and the light bulb still burnt out.

Question. What is the potential difference now across the burnt-out bulb?

Answer. It is impossible to say what the potential difference across the burnt-out light bulb is when the switch is open. The reason is that the bottom part of the circuit containing the resistor is now disconnected from the rest of the circuit containing the battery.

The potential must be constant along the bottom part because there is no current here and because the resistance is finite everywhere. However, one cannot say what this constant value is unless one knows the capacitances of the burnt-out light bulb and the open switch. This information is usually unavailable. (Don't worry if you don't know what a capacitance is. This question was added only to show that not all questions can be answered easily.)

If you click on any grid point in the bottom part of the circuit, the program will display a potential with a question mark.
