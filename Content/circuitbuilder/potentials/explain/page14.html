Electric Potentials and Potential Differences

Answer: The potential difference across the burnt-out light bulb is 100 V. The reason is that the filament of the bulb is broken so that there is now a gap in the circuit and the light bulb acts like an open switch. The situation is analogous to that discussed for the open switch on p.9. The potential difference across the bulb is equal to the battery emf, i.e., 100 V. This way the sum of the potential differences around the circuit is zero. (100 V is the magnitude of the potential difference across the burnt-out bulb. The actual potential change across the bulb would be -100 V if we went around the circuit clockwise.)
