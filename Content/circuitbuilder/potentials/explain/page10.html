Electric Potentials and Potential Differences

Now that you know the basics about electric potentials in DC circuits, you are ready to tackle a situation that may be a bit tricky at first.  The circuit shown on the left has a switch inserted in the wires on the right.  The switch is closed so that there is a current in the circuit.  Now open the switch by selecting the Open Switch button in the display area below the circuit.  When you have done this correctly, the switch will be open and the current will have stopped.

Question:  What is the change in potential as you go across the switch when it is open?  Or, in other words, what is the 'potential difference' across the switch?  Potential difference means the difference VB - VA where A is a point on one side of the switch and B a point on the other side.

The answer will be given on the next page, but try to think of the answer yourself, with reasons that are based on the understanding of the electric potential developed on pages 1 to 7.

You may want to find the answer by connecting a voltmeter to measure the potential difference across the switch.  If you do that, you still should try to explain what you observe.
