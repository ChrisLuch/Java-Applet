Electric Potentials and Potential Differences

Answer: The change in potential as you go from point A to point B across the resistor is minus 12 V,

VB - VA = 0 - 12 = -12 V.

The potential changes by -12 V rather than +12 V because change is defined as 'final minus initial value'.  In going from A to B, not from B to A, the potential changes from 12 V to 0 V.  The potential decreases and changes by -12 V.

Question: Thus, as one goes across a resistor in the direction of the current, the potential decreases.  Why?

Hint: You need to consider how the electric potential energy of an electric charge changes as it moves through the resistor.

Once you know what happens to the electric potential energy, you also know how the electric potential changes, if you know how the two quantities are related.  To review this background, see Electric Potential under Get Information.
