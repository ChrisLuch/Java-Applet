Fluid flow analogue of electric potential

The simulation shows fluid flowing in two pipes of different diameters and slopes. The two pipes are a segment of a complete circuit. The fluid has different speeds in the two adjoining pipes, faster in the narrower pipe, so that the current (fluid mass per second passing a given point) is the same in the two pipes.

As the fluid moves down the pipes, the gravitational potential energy of the fluid decreases. (The loss in potential energy is equal to the heat generated in the pipes.)

Definition. The gravitational potential at any point P is equal to the gravitational potential energy of a particle of mass m at that point divided by the mass m. The gravitational potential is independent of m.

Question. What is the gravitational potential in the pipes at points with elevation y = 10 m, y = 3 m, and y = 2 m? Take the acceleration of gravity, g, to have the value 10 m s-2 to make the numbers simple. When done, go on to the next page.
