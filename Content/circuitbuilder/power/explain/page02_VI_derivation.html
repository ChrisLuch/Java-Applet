Derivation of P = (DV)I

Suppose there is a resistor R within an electric DC circuit and that there is a current I in and a potential difference DV across the resistor. You may imagine the resistor to be the resistor R1 in the circuit on the left with current and potential difference as shown in the dircuit diagram.

At any given instant, there will be an amount of movable charge Q present in the resistor. It will take an amount of time t for this charge to move through the resistor. Then the current I is given by

I = Q/t .

In moving through the resistor, the charge Q loses an amount of electrical potential energy DPE equal to

DPE = Q(DV).

This much energy is dissipated in the resistor in the form of thermal energy. (The resistor gets warm.) Thus, the rate of energy dissipation, the dissipated power P, is equal to

P = (DPE)/t = Q(DV)/t = (DV)(Q/t) = (DV)I .
