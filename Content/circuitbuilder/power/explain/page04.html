Power in Electric Circuits

Answer. More power is dissipated in the top circuit than in the bottom circuit, twice as much in fact.

Reason. The two resistors in the bottom circuit are in-series. Therefore, their combined resistance is 2R reducing the current in the bottom circuit to half that in the top circuit, as shown by the ammeters.

Let V0 denote the battery emf. V0 is also the potential difference across the resistor in the top circuit and across the combination of two resistors in the bottom circuit, if the internal resistance of the battery is negligible, which we will assume.

Then the power dissipated in the top circuit is

Ptop = V02/R

and in the bottom circuit it is

Pbottom = V02/(2R) = (1/2)Ptop.
