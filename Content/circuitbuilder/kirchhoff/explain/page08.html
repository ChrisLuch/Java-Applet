The Wheatstone Bridge

A combination of five resistors like that shown on the left is used in a resistance measuring apparatus called Wheatstone Bridge. You may want to try to break down this circuit into in-series and in-parallel combinations of resistors. It should prove impossible to do. To calculate the currents in the different branches of the circuit we need to use Kirchhoff's Laws.

There are six branches, the branch containing the battery and the five branches containing one of the five resistors each. Let us denote the currents in the six branches by I0 to I5, where I0 is the current in branch 0 containing the battery and I1 to I5 are the currents in branches 1 to 5 containing the correspondingly labeled resistors.

We need six equations to solve for the six unknown currents. Try to write down three current equations and three voltage equations that are independent of each other. When done, go on to Page 9 to compare.
