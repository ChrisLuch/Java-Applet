The resistance of each one of the three resistors is equal to 2 W .

Solution

Plan It. The value of the total resistance Rtot of the combination of the three resistors can be calculated from the values of the battery terminal potential difference and the current in the circuit.

This value can be expressed in terms of R, the resistance of one of the resistors. (Use the equations for resistors in series and in parallel in Resistance under Get Info.)

Set It Up and Solve It. From the definition of resistance,

Rtot = (12 V)/(4 A) = 3 W .

The group of three resistors consists of a combination of two resistors in parallel that is in series with the remaining resistor. The resistance Rpar of the combination of the two in-parallel resistors is R/2. (The calculation is done at the end.)

The remaining resistor is in series with Rpar. Therefore,

Rtot = R + Rpar = R + R/2 = 1.5 R .

1.5 R = 3 W implies R = 2 W.

Think About It. The circuit contains three resistors, each of which has a resistance of 2 W. The resistance of the entire combination is, however, not 3x2 = 6 W, but only 3 W. Resistances, in general, don't simply add.

Resistance of the two in-parallel resistors.

1/Rpar = 1/R + 1/R = 2/R .

Thus, Rpar = R/2.
