Voltage Dividers

The simulation shows a 4-W and an 8-W resistor in series. According to the theorem on Page 6, the resistance of this series combination is 4 + 8 = 12 W. This is born out by the ammeter reading of 1.0 A. The current in the resistors should be equal to the ratio (total potential difference)/(total resistance) = (12 V)/(12 W) = 1 A.

Question. How would you calculate the potential differences across the resistors as shown by the voltmeters?

Hint: According to Kirchhoff's loop rule (voltage law), the potential differences across two resistors in series should add up to the total potential difference across the series combination, which in this case is equal to the battery emf of 12 V. This is one condition. Can you think of another condition that the two potential differences must satisfy? (Note that the currents in the voltmeters are negligible because voltmeters have very high internal resistances. Thus, the presence of the meters does not affect the conditions in the circuit.)
