The Concept of Resistance

Answer. The tanker truck at the top will fill twice as fast as the one at the bottom. The reason is that the elevation change of the top pipe is twice that of the bottom pipe. This causes the water to move twice as fast in the top pipe. Water current is proportional to change in elevation.

However, the current in the pipe does not only depend on the change in elevation along the pipe. It also depends on how much resistance there is to water flow through the pipe. A pipe with smaller diameter will allow less water to flow per unit time, for a given change in elevation. One says it has more resistance.

One defines the resistance, R, of a pipe by

I = (1/R) DV.

Here, I is the current and DV is the change in gravitational potential. (Gravitational potential is the same as the change in elevation except for a constant factor. See Gravitational Potential under Get Info.)

Note that this equation says that I is proportional to DV, which is as it should be. However, since DV is divided rather than multiplied by R, is defined so that I is inversely proportional to R. This is so because resistance should be defined so that for a larger resistance one gets a smaller current.
