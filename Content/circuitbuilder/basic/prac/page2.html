DC Circuits

Circuit. A basic circuit is a loop in which some substance can circulate. In the fluid flow example on the left, fluid circulates up through the pump, down the inclined narrow pipe, and back to the pump in the wide horizontal pipe.

In the analogous electric circuit shown here, the circulating 'fluid' consists of electrically charged particles. The pump is a battery, and the pipes are conducting metal wires, the narrow pipe being the high-resistance wire of a heating coil (as in a toaster).

Conductors are materials containing movable charged particles. Metals are the most common example. They contain movable negatively charged electrons. In a solid metal, the atoms that give up the movable electrons have fixed positions. They become positively charged ions after giving up an electron to the movable electron fluid. Overall, the metal is neutral.
