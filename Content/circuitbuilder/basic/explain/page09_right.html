Electric Circuits

All circuits on the preceding pages were simple loops. Circuits can also have branches. An example of a fluid flow circuit with two parallel branches, the pipes labeled 'c', is illustrated on the left above. The circuit diagram for an analogous electric circuit is illustrated on the right.

Both circuits contain four resistive segments, labeled 'a', 'b', 'c' and 'c'. Because of mass or charge conservation, the current in each of the identical segments c must be half that in segment a or b. The current in segment b is split into two equal portions upon leaving segment b and entering the two segments c.
