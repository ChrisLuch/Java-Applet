Light Bulbs Continued

The circuit now contains a light bulb in series with a resistor.  There is no current in the circuit because the switch is open, and the bulb does not glow.

Close the switch (click on the Select Tool, then on the switch; then deselect Open Switch).  The bulb should light up.

Now double the emf of the battery. (See p.6 on how to do this.)  Observe the change in brightness of the light bulb.  Try to find the emf values at which the bulb either burns out or gets dimmed to the point where it is no longer giving off light.

Try to vary the light output of the bulb by changing the resistance of the resistor.

You can also change the design voltage and power of the bulb (click on the Select Tool, then the bulb; etc.).  Observe the changes in light output.
