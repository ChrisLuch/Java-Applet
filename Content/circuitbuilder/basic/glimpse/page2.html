Electric Circuits

Circuits. The essential thing about a circuit is that it is a closed path. Whatever it is that is moving in a circuit must follow this closed path and eventually return to the starting point and continue on from there. Any point along the circuit can be thought of as the starting point.

In the case of the hydrologic cycle, let's take the point where the two people are as the starting point. The stuff that is moving is the water. It goes downstream from there into the ocean, evaporates and rises, moves inland, falls down in the form of rain or snow, and eventually passes the starting point again, or comes down another river. (The hydrologic cyle is not the simplest kind of circuit consisting of a single loop. Instead, in contains many parallel branches. Electric circuits too can contain parallel branches.)

The stuff that is moving in an electric circuit is particles that carry electric charge, e.g., electrons.
