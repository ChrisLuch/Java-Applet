Propagation of Error
Max/Min Method

The applet illustrates an example of a kind of problem that arises frequently in error analysis: One has measured some quantities and determined their uncertainties and then wants to calculate other quantities from the measured ones and wants to know the uncertainties of the calculated quantities. In the case of the applet, there is only one measured quantity, the radius, and only one calculated quantity, the area of the circle.

The task of determining the error in a calculated quantity from given errors in measured quantities is called error propagation.

The easiest way of handling error propagation is to use the max/min method. This is the method used in the applet. One simply determines the largest and smallest values of the calculated quantity that are possible given the uncertainty ranges of the measured quantities.
