Applying Abs. & Rel. Error

The distinction between absolute and relative uncertainties has practical consequences when calculating the uncertainties of combined quantities.

Suppose you have measured a distance traveled and a time elapsed and have calculated the corresponding average speed by dividing distance traveled by time elapsed. You also have values for the uncertainties in the distance traveled and time elapsed and want to calculate the corresponding uncertainty in the average speed. In a case like this, where a ratio is involved, this is done most easily by working with relative uncertainties.

In other situations, where you need to add or subtract measurement values, calculating the uncertainty in the sum or difference is done most easily by using absolute uncertainties.

Combining uncertainties is covered under another heading: Errors/Propagation of Error.
