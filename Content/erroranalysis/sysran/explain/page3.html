Systematic Error in Experiments

Here is another reaction time experiment. Imagine that you are measuring the length of time it takes for the sound of a firecracker to reach you from far away. This is the time elapsed between the moment you see the explosion and the moment the sound reaches you.

Click the button to light the firecracker, and click again when you "hear" the sound. There will be a visual cue as to when this happens, in the form of a large, red "BANG" under the picture.

You can't know ahead of time when the sound will arrive at your ears. This means your ear has to tell your brain you heard a sound, and your brain has to tell your finger to hit the "I heard it!" button. So there will be some reaction time.

For comparison, the average reaction time from the color change experiment is given along with your reaction time for the firecracker. They should be similar -- essentially, the same mechanism is at work inside your brain and central nervous system. These two experiments can give you an idea about how much systematic error there might be in an experiment in which you have to operate a stopwatch.
