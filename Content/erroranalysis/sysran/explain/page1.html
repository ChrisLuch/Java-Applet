Systematic vs. Random Error

On the left is a timer, "attached" to a red square. When the Start Timer button is clicked, the square turns yellow and a random amount of time will pass before the square turns red again. As soon as you see that happen, click the Reaction button (same button as before). If you click too early, when the square is still yellow and the Reaction button still inactive, your click will not be recorded.

Repeat the trial. After each trial, your reaction time will be displayed. This is the amount of time elapsed between the moment the square turns red and the Reaction button is clicked. It is not not the time elapsed between the two button clicks.

There are ten trials in total. When you have completed them, the button below the square will turn into an Analyze button. By clicking it, you can display a summary of your reaction times. On the following pages, these data will be related to the systematic error and random error in measuring the time interval during which the square stays yellow.
