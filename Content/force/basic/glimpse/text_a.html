(a) Circular Motion

The red particle is interacting with a larger yellow object. When you PLAY the motion, you can observe that while the red particle is performing circular motion, the yellow object is performing another circular motion with a much smaller radius.

This kind of motion can be observed when a planet (red) is revolving around the sun (yellow). The planet and the sun are interacting gravitationally, and while the sun exerts a gravitational force on the planet the planet in turn exerts a gravitational force on the sun.

Comment. In reality, the motion of the sun would be much less than that shown here because the sun is so much more massive than a planet. Also, the orbits of planets are slightly elliptical, not circular as shown here, and the sun's orbit is non-circular as well.
