Newton's Third Law

The equation

a1/a2 = m2/m1

implies that the acceleration of object 1 becomes very, very small if the mass of object 1 becomes very much larger than the mass of object 2.

The simulation on the left illustrates this. In this case, object 1 is the earth and object 2 is an apple. You can only see a tiny part of the earth, the part right under the apple tree. Click START and watch the motions of the apple and the earth resulting from their gravitational interaction.

The apple accelerates downward towards the earth, but the upward acceleration of the earth is not noticeable, because the mass of the earth is so much larger than that of the apple.

Nevertheless, as the simulation on Pages 2 to 5 shows, the apple exerts a gravitational force on the earth that is equal in magnitude to the gravitational force that the earth exerts on the apple. If the apple weighs 2 N, it exerts an upward 2-N force on the earth. You can imagine this force to be acting at the center of the earth.

The gravitational forces that the apple and the earth are exerting on each other are an action-reaction pair of forces.
