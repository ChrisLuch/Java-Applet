Newton's Third Law

The gravitational forces acting on the two objects are equal to each other at all times, although they are steadily increasing during the motion. You may want to repeat the experiment and stop the motion to compare the magnitudes of the two forces at that instant.

Also, both objects are accelerating. This shows that each object has a force exerted on it.
