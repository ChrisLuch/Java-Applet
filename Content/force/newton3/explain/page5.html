Newton's Third Law

The larger object has the smaller acceleration. It's really the mass, not the size, that is important, but the objects here are assumed to have the same mass density, which means that the larger object also has more mass.

It follows from Newton's Second Law that F1 = m1a1 where a1 is the magnitude of the acceleration of object 1. An analogous equation holds for object 2. Together with Newton's Third Law, which requires F1 = F2, it follows that

m1a1 = m2a2,

at every instant during the motion. Thus, at every instant,

a1/a2 = m2/m1.

Thus, if m1/m2 > 1, i.e., if object 1 has the greater mass, then a1/a2 = m2/m1 < 1, i.e., object 1 has the smaller acceleration. Do the experiment and verify this.
