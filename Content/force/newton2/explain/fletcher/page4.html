Acceleration in Fletcher's Trolley

The third equation is the equality of the two tensions,

T1 = T2 = T,(3)

where the symbol T has been introduced to denote the common value of the two tensions. The tensions in a string on the two sides of a massless, frictionless pulley are equal, by the pulley theorem.

Step 4. We now need to 'turn the crank' and solve the equations. Setting T1 = T2 = T in equations (1) and (2) leaves us with two equations for the two unknowns a and T. Try to solve these equations yourself before going on to Page 5.
