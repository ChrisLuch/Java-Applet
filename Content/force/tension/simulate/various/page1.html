Tension Simulations

Preparation: Force/Tension/Explain It.

On the next page you can access the five simulations from the session named above.

  • Vertical Rope. Tension at different points in a massless rope. The rope is suspended from a ceiling and holds up a crate of variable mass.
  • Vertical Massive Rope. Tension at different points in a massive rope suspended from a ceiling. The mass of the rope can be varied.
  • Tug of War. Tension at different points in a horizontal stationary rope that has several forces applied to it at either end.
  • Horizontal Rope. Tension at different points in a horizontal stationary rope. The rope has a single force applied to it at either end whose magnitude can be varied.
  • Train Demo. The tensions in the strings connecting three blocks pulled on a horizontal surface by an applied force. The system can have friction. The applied force, the masses of the blocks, and the friction coefficients can be varied.

For explanations of how to use the simulations, please go to Force/Tension/Explain It.
