Tension - What Is It?

The horizontal case shown here corresponds to the tug-of-war situation from Page 1. There, we had a rope that had a 1300-N force applied at its left end pulling to the left and another 1300-N force at the right end pulling to the right. Here, the common magnitude of these forces can be adjusted by the slider. Again, 'cut' the rope at various points along its length.

What do you conclude about the forces exerted at any point along the rope by one piece of rope on the other? And about their magnitudes, the tension?

So - the tension is the same everywhere along the rope, and it is equal to the magnitude of one of the external forces applied to the rope at its ends. If there is a 300-N force applied to the rope at the left end and an opposite 300-N force applied to the rope at the right end, the tension in the rope is 300 N.

In the tug-of-war example from Page 1, the tension in the rope between the teams is 1300 N.
