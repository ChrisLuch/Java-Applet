Tension in a Dynamic Situation

The examples on the preceding pages were about tension in static situations. The simulation above illustrates tension in a non-static situation.

You can vary the masses of the blocks via the three sliders on the left. You can apply a force to the 'train' of blocks by dragging the front end of the front string to the right. The train will accelerate. The three tension dials show the tensions in the three strings. The strings are assumed to be massless.

You can watch the motion either from the Lab frame (LF) or the frame in which the blocks are at rest (BF) by clicking either the LF or BF button. You can freeze the motion by clicking the PAUSE/PLAY button. The tensions will continue to be displayed as before.

Vary the masses and the applied force and observe the changes in the tensions. Can you explain the tension values in terms of Newton's laws applied to the system? Please note that in the present simulation the blocks have no friction.
