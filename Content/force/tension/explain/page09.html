Variable Tension

In the last example, the tension was not the same everywhere. Here we have another example where the tension is variable, a massive rope suspended from a ceiling. The tension is due to the gravitational forces acting on the various parts of the rope.

Use the slider to set the weight of the rope to the value you want. Then move the arrow to the point in the rope where you want to test the tension and click the scissors icon.

Try to explain how the tension varies with position, using Newton's second law. Then go on to the next page and compare.
