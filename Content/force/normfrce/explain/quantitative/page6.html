Normal Force

Summary

  • When an object is in vertical accelerated motion, the normal force acting on the object is not equal in magnitude to the weight of the object. The normal force can be calculated by applying Newton's second law to the object.

  • If an object rests on a bathroom scale in an accelerating elevator, the scale measures the normal force applied by the scale to the object, not the object's weight.

Experiment with the simulation and see if you can predict what you observe. Predict the value of N when the 2.0-kg object moves with

  • downward acceleration and upward velocity,
  • upward acceleration and downward velocity,
  • upward acceleration and upward velocity.
