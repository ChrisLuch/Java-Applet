Torque About an Axis

Although the torque due to a force depends on

  1. the magnitude of the force
  2. the direction of the force
  3. the reference point
  4. the point at which the force is acting,

it does not depend on items 2 to 4 independently, but depends on them only through the lever arm. If you select SHOW LEVER ARM, the lever arm for the given configuration will be shown in green.

If the letter l denotes the lever arm, F the magnitude of the force, and t (read: "tau") the torque, then

t = (sign) Fl .

In this equation it is assumed that the force vector lies in the plane perpendicular to the rotation axis, as in our simulation. Otherwise, we would have to consider the component of the force in this plane. A force component parallel to the rotation axis would not contribute to the torque about this axis.

Observe how the lever arm changes when you change one of the items 2 to 4. However, if several of these are varied such that the lever arm and the sign do not change, the torque does not change.

For more information on the lever arm, go to Torque/Lever Arm/Explain It. Also see the Get Information item.
