Torque About an Axis

What is unrealistic about the present simulation?

The purpose of the present simulation is to illustrate the definition of torque about an axis, to give an indication of when the torque is large or small, and to show when it is positive or negative. This is done by pretending the axis about which the torque is taken is fixed. The corresponding rotation is shown with an angular speed that is larger if the net torque is larger and with a sense corresponding to the sign of the torque.

The simulated motion is unrealistic in several respects:

  • It is a rotation about a fixed axis through O even though in reality there may be no fixed axis through O and the motion of the object may be quite different.
  • It has constant angular speed and therefore zero angular acceleration, while in reality a non-zero net torque produces non-zero angular acceleration.
  • It stops as soon as the object has rotated to the point where the net torque is zero. In reality, the motion would overshoot this point and then oscillate around it, assuming that the forces don't change and that they continue to act at the same points in the body.
