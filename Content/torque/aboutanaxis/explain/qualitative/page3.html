Torque About an Axis

Make a single force act on the object and then click the PLAY button. Barring exceptional circumstances, the object will rotate. One says that the force creates a torque and that it is this torque that makes the object rotate. Sometimes a non-zero force will create a zero torque. Then the object will not rotate. Try to find an arrangement like this.

The torque (about an axis) is a scalar that has a sign and a magnitude. It is important to understand what determines these two attributes of a torque.

Sign of torque. Let a single force be acting on the object. Arrange things so that the torque is positive and observe the sense of rotation. Then arrange things so that the torque is negative and observe the sense of rotation.

If a torque makes an object rotate counter-clockwise, one defines the torque to be positive. The torque is negative if it makes the object rotate clockwise.
