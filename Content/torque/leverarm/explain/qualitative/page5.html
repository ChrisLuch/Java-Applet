The Lever Arm

Point B where the 200-N force is acting has been moved to the right compared to where it was on Page 4. The beam is now balanced. Check it by clicking RELEASE.

The lever arm is indicated in green. The blue line indicates the lever arm that we had on Page 3, where the system was balanced when the 200-N force was vertical. Note that the green and blue lines have the same lengths. That must be so if both times the 200-N force is to balance the 100-kg weight. It must have the same torque at both times. (Torque = force x lever arm.)

When first learning about torques and lever arms, students often take the line segment from the rotation axis to the point where the force is applied, O to B in the present situation, as the lever arm. This is not the lever arm when the force has the direction shown. The lever arm is shorter than the the segment OB.

The lever arm is always perpendicular to the force.
