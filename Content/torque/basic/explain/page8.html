Torque and Rotation

On Pages 1 to 7, you could observe how an object will respond to one or two applied forces if the object is allowed to move freely. On the remaining two pages, you will be able to observe the response of a constrained object, in this case an object that is constrained to rotate around a fixed axis.

The object is a physical pendulum consisting of a flat disk rigidly attached to a rigid massless rod. The pendulum can oscillate in the plane of the screen around an axis perpendicular to the screen through the fixed point O.

When you select button 1, the forces acting on the pendulum will be displayed by red arrows. One force acting on the pendulum is the gravitational force. The gravitational force can be thought of as acting at the object's CM. Will there be any other forces acting on the pendulum? What do you think?

Question: What would be the pendulum's motion if the gravitational force were the only force acting on it?

Answer: The pendulum would fall straight down without any change in the orientation of the rod. The motion would be like that of the object on Page 4, if you set the initial velocity of that object equal to zero. (Drag the red velocity vector until its length is zero.)

Of course, this is not what will happen to the pendulum if its rod continues to be supported at O. Now click the PLAY button, with button 1 selected so that you can observe both the motion of the pendulum and the forces acting on the pendulum.

Are the two forces that are acting on the pendulum, the gravitational force acting at the CM and the force that the axle support exerts on the rigid rod at point O, consistent with the pendulum's oscillatory motion?

You may want to increase the radius of the disk substantially, to something like 4 m. Use the data entry field for the radius. You can also drag the bob to a different initial position and change the length l of the pendulum this way. The magnitude and direction of the constraint force, especially at the moment when the pendulum is released, will become more apparent when you make the bob's radius very large.
