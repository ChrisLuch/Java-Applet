Torque and Rotation

The motion of any rigid object can be thought of as a combination of a translational motion of the CM of the object and a rotation of the object around the CM.

You may never have thought of a pendulum's motion this way. It seems much easier to think of this motion as a pure rotation around the axis through O. Nevertheless, it is instructive to think of the pendulum's motion as a translation of the CM and rotation around the CM. Only this point of view will allow you to understand and calculate the force of constraint exerted by the axle on the rod at point O.

The force of constraint creates a torque about the CM that causes the pendulum bob to rotate about the CM. The gravitational force does not create a torque about the CM and does not make the bob rotate around the CM.

In addition, the CM undergoes accelerated translational motion. This acceleration is determined by the net force, the sum of the gravitational force and the constraint force exerted by the axle. Without the constraint force, the bob's acceleration would be vertically downward. You can display the acceleration of the CM by selecting button 2. The acceleration is represented by the blue arrow. It is anything but downward! (The green arrow represents the velocity of the CM.)

Note that the force of constraint exerted by the axle on the rod is a complicated force that changes both in direction and magnitude during the pendulum's motion.
