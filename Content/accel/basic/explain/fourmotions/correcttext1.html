(d) Constant Velocity Motion

Yes, motion (d) is the only motion in which the object has zero acceleration.

To start the motion, click anywhere in the simulation window. To reset, click a second time.

The object is moving in a straight line with constant speed. One also says that it is moving with constant velocity.

For the acceleration to be non-zero, the velocity must be changing, either its magnitude or its direction. Here neither the magnitude nor the direction of the velocity are changing. Thus the acceleration is zero.
