Acceleration

Like velocity, the acceleration of an object is an instantaneous quantity that can vary from one instant to another. One can therefore refer to it as instantaneous acceleration. Usually, however, one simply says 'acceleration'.

Since acceleration is a vector quantity, it is denoted by a vector symbol, usually a. There is no special name for the magnitude of the acceleration. (The magnitude of the velocity has the special name 'speed'.) The magnitude of the acceleration is a scalar and denoted by the letter a.

One also needs the concept of average acceleration. This is an average of the (instantaneous) acceleration over a finite time interval. It is a vector denoted aav.

This session has presented an intuitive, qualitative understanding of acceleration.
