Acceleration

Have you asked yourself what the motion of an object in Position Space would look like that has the accelerations and velocities simulated on Pages 5 and 6? The simulation will let you observe this motion as follows.

Click the toggle button labelled 'Object'. A disk will be placed on the screen. Clicking the button again, will make the disk disappear. You can drag the disk anywhere in the applet window. This disk exists in Position Space which you can imagine to be placed on top of Velocity Space, transparently so that one can see Velocity Space underneath Position Space.

You can set the velocity vector to an initial value and then control the motion with the acceleration dial as before. The disk will move accordingly. E.g., how about trying to make the disk perform projectile motion? Hint: Place the disk in the lower left-hand corner of the screen, set the initial velocity to point upward and to the right, and then choose a constant acceleration vector that points straight down. Observe the way the velocity changes during the motion.

As on Page 6, you can click REPLAY and then step through the motion. In the REPLAY mode, the acceleration vector will be shown attached to the disk. Observe that, in any kind of motion, the acceleration either points to the inside of the disk's trajectory or along the tangent to the trajectory, but never away from the trajectory to the outside of the trajectory. Why not?
