Acceleration

The simulation shows the velocity (magenta) and acceleration (orange) vectors of a swinging pendulum. As you can see, they are constantly changing and, in general, point in different directions at any given instant.

This session will provide an intuitive, qualitative explanation of how velocity and acceleration are related. Both of them are vector quantities, and it is important to understand that acceleration is derived from velocity, which is a vector, and not from speed, which is a scalar. This fact is at the core of some essential properties of acceleration.
