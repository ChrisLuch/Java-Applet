Acceleration

Like velocity, the acceleration of an object is an instantaneous quantity that can vary from one instant to another. One can therefore refer to it as instantaneous acceleration. Usually, however, one simply says 'acceleration'.

This session has presented an intuitive, qualitative understanding of acceleration. Next you need to study the quantitative definition of acceleration and certain quantitative results about acceleration. To do so, it is helpful to learn about average acceleration first. Thus, it is recommended that you go to Acceleration/Ave. and Instant., then to Acceleration/Tangential, then to Acceleration/Radial (centripetal).

If you want to experiment more with the simulations used in this session, go to Acceleration/Basic Facts/Simulate It.
