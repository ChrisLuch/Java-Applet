(a) Circular Motion

To start the motion, click anywhere in the simulation window. To reset, click a second time.

The object (red) is interacting with a second object (yellow) while performing circular motion.

This kind of motion can be observed when a planet is revolving around the sun, although in that case the motion of the yellow object (sun) would be much less than shown here because the sun is so much more massive than a planet.

Note that the yellow object is revolving around a fixed point, just like the red object. In fact, both objects are revolving around the same fixed point, namely, the center of mass of this two-body system.
