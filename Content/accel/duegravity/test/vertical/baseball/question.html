Baseball

Conceptual questions on free fall and motion on an incline without friction.

A baseball pitcher throws a ball straight up into the air.  The ball eventually turns around and falls back down.  Neglect air resistance.  Which of the following statements is correct?

After release from the pitcher's hand, the ball's acceleration

  1. is downward at all times: on the way up, right at the top, and on the way down.

  2. is upward at first, but decreases on the way up until it reaches zero at the top, then is downward on the way down.

  3. on the way up is greater than the acceleration due to gravity and is directed upward, on the way down it is equal to the acceleration due to gravity and directed downward.

  4. has a magnitude less than the acceleration due to gravity on the way up, is zero at the top, and is equal in magnitude to the acceleration due to gravity on the way down.
