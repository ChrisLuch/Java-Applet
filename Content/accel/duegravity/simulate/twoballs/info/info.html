Simulation of Projectile Motion With or Without Air Resistance

You can observe two balls simultaneously performing projectile motion. The initial positions and velocities of the two balls can be set separately. This way you can compare the motions of two balls with different initial conditions.

You can also compare the motion of a ball that is affected by air resistance (yellow ball) to that of a ball not affected by air resistance (red ball). The amount of air resistance acting on the yellow ball can be adjusted by varying the air resistance constant C between 0 and 0.02 kg/m by means of a slider.

The larger the value of C, the more air resistance there is. The x and y components of the yellow ball's acceleration, ax and ay, are equal to (the x-axis is horizontal, the y-axis points vertically upward):

ax = -(C/m)vvx and ay = -g - (C/m)vvy,

where v is the ball's speed, vx and vy the x and y-components of the ball's velocity, respectively, g the magnitude of the acceleration due to gravity, and m the ball's mass.

The yellow ball's acceleration is a combination of the constant downward acceleration due to gravity and a part due to air resistance whose magnitude is proportional to the square of the ball's speed and whose direction is opposite to the ball's velocity.

You can vary the mass m of the yellow ball and observe how such changes affect the ball's motion. (The red ball's motion is unaffected by changes in that ball's mass because the ball experiences no air resistance.)

You can vary the magnitude g of the acceleration due to gravity. This will affect both balls, and you can observe the resultant changes in the balls' motions.

The position coordinates of the two balls are printed out during the motion vs. the time elapsed. Also shown are the current direction angles of the balls' velocities.

You can also observe the following:

  • the balls' velocity vectors - ball 1 in purple, ball 2 in yellow; always displayed
    • the ball's velocity components - the data entry fields for the x and y components of the velocities of the two balls are continually being updated during the balls' motions
  • the balls' trajectories - in black; to display, click the Trace toggle button.

You have the following controls available:

  • the balls' initial positions - drag the balls
  • purely vertical movement of the balls - hold down the shift key while dragging a ball
  • y=0 level (x-axis) - drag the tip of the x-axis up or down at the right end of the axis
  • coordinate grid - square grid; to display, click the Grid toggle button.
  • the balls' initial velocities - either drag the tip of the velocity vector of a ball or type different values in the velocity data fields (x and y components of velocity of the ball) and then hit RETURN
  • setting a target - select the T toggle button and then click at the desired target location; the target's x,y coordinates are displayed as long as the mouse button is depressed; the target can be dragged; the target can be erased by clicking on the target a second time
  • displaying the x,y coordinates of a point - select the C button and then click on the point; the point can be dragged; the display of the coordinates can be erased by clicking on the point a second time
  • motion control - clicking the PLAY button will run the ball's motion continuously until you click the same button again and the motion is paused; clicking the STEP button allows you to view the motion in discrete time steps, one step at a time; a slider lets you vary the size of the steps
  • reset and repeat - clicking the RESET button restores the applet to its default setting; clicking the REPEAT button restores the applet to the initial setting chosen by the user; the user can change settings after either RESET or REPEAT.
