(d) Constant Velocity Motion

When you PLAY the motion, the red particle moves with constant speed in a straight line. One also says that it moves with constant velocity.

The particle is not interacting with anything.

People did not always think of constant-velocity motions as interactionless. They thought that some agent (force) was required to keep things moving, even with constant velocity.

Observations from daily life support this kind of thinking. Wherever we see a motion with constant velocity it is maintained by some kind of an agent. Think of cars. Don't they require an engine to keep them moving with constant velocity?

The Aristotelean view that an agent (interaction) is required to maintain a constant-velocity motion was generally accepted for almost 2000 years until the sixteenth century AD when it was challenged by Galileo. Galileo proposed a law of inertia that stated a contrary view: an object rolling on a perfectly smooth earth would keep on rolling forever, unless acted upon by some agent.
