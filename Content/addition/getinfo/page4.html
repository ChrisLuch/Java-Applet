Vector Addition

 Equivalence Of The Two Addition Methods

The diagram at the bottom illustrates why the tip-to-tail method gives the same result as the parallelogram method. The reason is that the vectors that form the opposite sides of a parallelogram are equal. It also illustrates that vector addition is commutative.

Notation

The sum of the two vectors is labelled +, not a + b. The latter would denote the sum of the magnitudes of the vectors, which is not the same as the sum of the vectors themselves.

Sum of Magnitudes vs. Magnitude of Sum

The magnitudes of the two vectors are scalars and their sum is again a scalar, represented by the sum of the lengths of the blue and orange arrows. This sum, denoted || + || = a + b, is not to be confused with the magnitude of the sum of the two vectors, |+|, which is represented by the length of the green arrow.
