Choice (a) is not correct. It represents a vector that is in the opposite direction from the sum of the two given vectors.

On a sheet of paper, draw the two vectors so that the tail of one vector is placed at the tip of the other vector. (You will have to draw the second vector a bit offset to one side, so that the two vectors aren't superimposed and you can see what you are doing.)

Then draw the arrow from the free tail of one of the vectors to the free tip of the other vector. This will represent the sum of the two vectors.
