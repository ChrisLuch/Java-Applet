Yes, "northwest at 30 m/s" is a complete specification of a car's velocity. It indicates both the direction and the magnitude of the velocity. Both of these attributes are essential in specifying a vector.

Note that there is another very convenient way to specify a vector, which is to specify a vector in terms of its components. (See Vectors/Scalar Components.) This is just as good as specifying the magnitude and direction. The point of saying that 'direction and magnitude both need to be specified' is that it is not enough to specify only one of these. The point is not that this is the only way of specifying a vector.
