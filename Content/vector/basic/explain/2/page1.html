Vector Specification

A vector in two dimensions needs two numbers for its specification. Four different ways (modes) of choosing these two numbers are demonstrated here, on four different pages.

1. Polar (positive). In this mode, a vector is specified by its magnitude and by an angle where the angle is measured from a reference line, shown dotted and pointing east, in the positive (counter-clockwise) sense. The angle is indicated by an arc. Angles can range from 0 to 360o. 360o is equivalent to 0o.
