Scalar Components of Vectors

Answer. Multiplying (or dividing) a vector by a scalar is accomplished by multiplying (or dividing) each of its components by the scalar. The vectors -2 and /2 are illustrated in the diagram. Their components are given underneath.

Question: Can you think of an example where you need to divide a vector by a scalar?

Answer: Suppose a car undergoes the displacement = (-3,4) m in 2 s. Then the car's average velocity during this time interval is

av = [(-3,4) m]/2 s

= (-1.5,2) m/s.
