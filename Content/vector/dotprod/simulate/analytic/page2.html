Dot or Scalar Product

The scalar product can be used to calculate the angle q spanned by two vectors and if the vectors are given in terms of their scalar components relative to a pair of Cartesian x and y axes.

First one calculates the value of the scalar product from

= axbx + ayby .

Then one inserts this value into the expression for the scalar product in terms of the magnitudes a and b of the two vectors and the angle q,

= ab cos q .

Combining both expressions for gives for q the equation

cos q = (axbx + ayby)/(ab) .

You can practise calculating q for different vectors. To change a vector, drag its tip in the applet on the left. The applet will display the vector's scalar components. After calculating q, check its value by clicking "Display Angle" in the grey panel. To check your intermediate values for the magnitudes of the two vectors, click "Display Magnitudes".
