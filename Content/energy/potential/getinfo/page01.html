Potential Energy

General Properties of Potential Energy

Potential energy is an energy of interaction between objects. For one object alone, there is no potential energy. There need to be at least two objects before there can be an interaction between objects and an associated potential energy. The second "object" can be a field, e.g., a gravitational field. Let us consider the case of just two interacting objects.

The potential energy of interaction between two objects depends on the separation of the two objects. In the most important cases, the potential energy depends only on the magnitude of the separation, although, in principle, a potential energy could also depend on the direction of the separation.

The two most fundamental types of interaction with which a potential energy can be associated are the gravitational and the electrostatic interactions. These two cases will now be considered on Pages 2 and 3. The general definition of potential energy underlying all of these will be given on Page 4.
