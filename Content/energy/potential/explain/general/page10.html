The Concept of Potential Energy

It turns out that the potential energy, and with it the mechanical energy, are not defined uniquely by DPE = -W. This is always a bit hard to understand at first because in daily life energy is treated as something very definite. E.g., one speaks of a daily energy intake of 2000 Calories. However, these 2000 Calories really refer to a change in chemical energy, the difference in chemical energy of the food materials before and after combustion.

We want to find a position-dependent expression, called potential energy PE, such that throughout the particles' motion

KE + PE = E = const .

Suppose we have found a suitable PE. Let us consider another potential energy expression, PE', obtained by adding a constant amount of energy to PE, say, 5 J,

PE' = PE + 5 J.

Obviously, with PE' instead of PE as the potential energy, we have

KE + PE' = E + 5 J = E' = const.

Thus, KE + PE' is constant also, and therefore PE' = PE + 5 J is a possible potential energy also. Of course, instead of 5 J we could have added any other constant value.

Thus, if we can find one possible PE, we can find infinitely many, all differing from each other by some constant.
