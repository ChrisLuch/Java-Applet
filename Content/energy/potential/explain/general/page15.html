The Concept of Potential Energy

Answer. Energy conservation implies that the initial energy of the ball-earth system, at the moment the ball leaves the hand, is equal to the final energy, at the moment on the way down when the motion is frozen. Thus,

(m/2)vi2 + mgyi = (m/2)vf2 + mgyf.

The subscripts 'i' and 'f' refer to the initial and final states. Note that all terms in this equation contain a common factor of m, which can therefore be canceled out of the equation. Then, rearranging the equation and substituting the given values vi = 8.0 m/s and Dy = yf - yi = 2.5 m, one gets

vf2 = vi2 - 2gDy = 8.02 - 2x9.8x2.5 = 15 m2/s2
