Potential Energy Quantitative

Question. On Page 5, three properties of the gravitational potential energy are specified. Let us find a mathematical expression that has all of these properties.

Let us solve this problem for the special case of a uniform gravitational force field, as we have near the surface of the earth. 'Uniform' means that the force has the same magnitude and direction everywhere. This is true for the gravitational force exerted on an object within a few hundred meters of the surface of the earth and within a horizontal range in which the earth's curvature is not significant.

Hints. The distance between the surface of the earth and the ball can be measured by the variable y indicated by the brown scale. We will see later that it is not important where the origin y = 0 is placed, but for the moment you may imagine it to be at the surface of the earth.

Look for an expression for PE that is a function of y.

When the kinetic energy of a ball increases by 20 J, its potential energy must decrease by 20 J. In other words, if DKE = 20 J, then DPE = -20 J. In general, if only the gravitational force is acting on the ball,

DPE = - DKE.

The work-kinetic energy theorem (see under Related Items) says that DKE is equal to the work done by the force of gravity on the ball. Use this fact to find an expression for DPE that satisfies the equation above. Then go on to Page 7 to compare.
