You picked choice (b), saying that the 0.6-kg block will move downward. It is tempting to say so because this block is heavier than the 0.4-kg block. However, the 0.4-kg block is on a steeper incline, which may compensate for its smaller weight.

The blocks will move so that their combined potential energy decreases as they move. They are in equilibrium if it does not change when they move.

Try to work out by how much the potential energy changes if both blocks move 1 m, in the same general direction, and determine if this change is positive, negative or zero.
