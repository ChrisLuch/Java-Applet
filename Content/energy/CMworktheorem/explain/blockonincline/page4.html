CM-Work Theorem Applied To A Block On An Incline

The CM-work WCM,fr done by the friction force on the block was calculated on Page 3 to be equal to -1.624 J. This is not equal to the real friction work done on the block. The real friction work is impossible to calculate because the displacements of the cold-welds between the block and the incline responsible for friction are unknown.

Nevertheless, it is possible to relate WCM,fr to the change ΔEtherm in thermal energy of the system consisting of the block and incline if one applies the law of conservation of energy to this system. It is not possible to say something about the increase in thermal energy of the block alone, or the incline alone, if one does not know the real friction work done on either.

The CM-work theorem applied to the block gives the equation

WCM,a + WCM,g = ΔKE - WCM,fr (1)

where KE is the block's translational kinetic energy.

The law of conservation of energy applied to the block-incline system gives the equation

Wa + Wg = ΔKE + ΔEtherm. (2)

Comments

  1. In Eq.(2), the work on the left-hand side is real work, in contrast to Eq.(1) where the work on the left-hand side is CM-work.

  2. The only energy input into the block-incline system is the work done by the applied and gravitational forces because these are the only external forces acting on the system. Friction is an internal force and therefore no friction work should be included on the left-hand side of Eq.(2).

  3. Friction is an external force when the system is taken to be just the block, and therefore the CM-work due to friction must be included in Eq.(1).

In the present case, where the block is not rotating, the displacements of the points at which the applied and gravitational forces are acting are equal to the displacements of the CM of the block. Therefore, the amounts of real work done by the applied and gravitational forces on the block are equal to the amounts of CM-work done by these forces on the block,

Wa = WCM,a and Wg = WCM,g. (3)

Thus, the left-hand sides of Eqs. (1) and (2) are equal. Therefore, the right-hand sides must be equal also, which implies

ΔEtherm = -WCM,fr = -(-1.624) = 1.624 J. (4)

Thus, the negative of the CM-work done on the block is equal to the change in the thermal energy of the block-incline system.
