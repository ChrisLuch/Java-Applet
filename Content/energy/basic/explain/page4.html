Energy - What Is It?

Question. Which ones of the properties of energy listed on Page 2 does the simulation on Page 3 illustrate? Try to list as many as you can think of.

Answer. The simulation can illustrate five of the six properties listed. The only one it does not illustrate is Property 6, that there are different kinds of energy transfer. Also note that the simulation treats energy as if it consisted of 1-J chunks while in reality energy is not split up like this.

So - what is energy? One can start by defining a quantity called kinetic energy. (See under Related Items.) The definitions of other forms of energy follow from there if one wants to define energy as a conserved quantity. (See Page 5 about the Principle of Energy Conservation.)

E.g., one can define the work done on a particle, i.e., the amount of mechanical energy transferred to the particle, so that the work is equal to the change in the particle's kinetic energy. Once the concept of work is defined, one can define potential energy, etc. For more on this, see Page 5.
