Energy Conservation in Fletcher's Trolley

Practise calculating the final speed a few more times, with different mass values and different initial and final heights of block 2. Then check your calculations with the simulation. You can vary the mass of block 2 with the slider. (The mass of block 1 is fixed at 0.6 kg.) You can vary the final height of block 2 by dragging the support of block 2 upward after first dragging block 1 left.

You can also vary the mass and therefore the moment of inertia of the pulley with another slider. This will result in a different final speed. In the calculation of the final speed you will need to add the pulley's kinetic energy to the kinetic energies of the two blocks. Thus, energy conservation will now give

KE1,in+KE2,in+KEp,in+PEin,

= KE1,fin+KE2,fin+KEp,fin+PEfin

where

KEp = (I/2)w2 = (I/2)(v/r)2

is the kinetic energy of the pulley, I the pulley's moment of inertia, w the pulley's angular speed, and r the pulley's radius.

For details and an example on pulley rotational kinetic energy in a similar system, please go to Energy/Energy Conservation/Explain It/Block and Pulley.
