Dv2 = -2 [ (m1/M) g Dh1 + (m2/M) g Dh2 ].

Our goal is to get an expression for the acceleration. The change in speed ought to be related to the acceleration. In fact, for motions along a straight line, there is a kinematical relation that relates Dv2 to the acceleration component along the line, which is just what we need:

Dv2 = 2 az Dz .

This relation holds only for constant acceleration, which is what we have here.

Substituting this expression into the equation at the top and dividing by 2 gives

az Dz = -(m1/M) g Dh1 - (m2/M) g Dh2.

Now, what do we do with the variable Dz? We can eliminate it from the equation after expressing the elevation changes Dh1 and Dh2 in terms of Dz.
