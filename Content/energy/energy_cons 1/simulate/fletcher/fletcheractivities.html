Energy Conservation in Fletcher's Trolley
Activities

Preparation: Energy/Energy Conserv'n/Explain It/Fletcher's Trolley.

Exercise 1. Set block 2 to some initial height and the platform below block 2 to some final height. Set the masses of the two blocks. Then allow block 2 to drop from rest from the initial height to the platform.

The system is frictionless.

Predict how the total mechanical energy E of the system and the potential energy and the kinetic energies will vary during the motion of the blocks, and then observe what happens in the simulation.

Repeat this with different masses for the blocks, and also with a massive pulley.

Exercise 2. Carry out the following calculations.

1. Using energy conservation, and the values of the masses of the blocks and the vertical displacement of block 2, predict the speed of block 2 just before the block hits the platform. Compare your value with that shown by the applet.

2. From the final speed found in Part 1, and the masses of the blocks and the magnitude of the acceleration due to gravity, determine the magnitude of the acceleration of the two blocks. Compare your value with that shown by the applet.

Repeat Parts 1 and 2 with a massive pulley. The moment of inertia of the pulley and the radius of the pulley are indicated on the pulley slider.
