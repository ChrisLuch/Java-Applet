Velocity

Final Remarks

Since velocity is a vector quantity, it is denoted by a vector symbol, often . The magnitude of the velocity, called speed, is a scalar denoted by the same letter as velocity but without an arrow, i.e., by v. The SI-unit of velocity is m/s.

The concept of average velocity is important also. This is an average of the (instantaneous) velocity over a finite time interval. It is a vector denoted av.

This session has presented an intuitive, qualitative understanding of velocity. Next you need to study the quantitative definition of velocity, already mentioned at the bottom of Page 5. For this, it is helpful to learn about average velocity first. Thus, it is recommended that you go to Velocity/Average Velocity next, and then to Velocity/Instantaneous Velocity.
